Users may download, print and share this work on the Internet for noncommercial purposes only, provided the original work is properly cited, and a link to the original work on http://www.karger.com and the terms of this license are included in any shared versions.Extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma (EMC) is a soft tissue malignancy characterized by specific chromosomal abnormalities involving the TEC gene. This disease has historically been considered largely indolent both histologically and clinically. Rarer subsets of EMC exist that demonstrate aggressive histopathologic features and clinical behavior, though it remains unclear whether or not aggressive histopathology is predictive of outcome. Herein we present a case of EMC with aggressive histopathologic features that underwent rapid clinical progression despite initial treatment with curative intent. This case provides the context for a discussion of the existing literature regarding treatment, prognosis, pathology, and genetic/molecular features of EMC in general and aggressive EMC specifically.
