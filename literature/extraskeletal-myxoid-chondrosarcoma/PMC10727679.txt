## Background:
Chondrosarcoma (CHS) is highly prone to recurrence and has become the most common malignant bone tumor in adults. The authors aim to identify and analyze the top 100 most-cited articles in this field, enabling researchers to quickly grasp the research focus and progress in the area of chondrosarcoma recurrence.

## Methods:
A search in the Web of Science database yielded a total of 305 articles related to CHS recurrence between 2013 and 2022. Filtering was done based on the titles and abstracts of the articles in the list, and the top 100 most-cited articles were selected. The following information were analyzed using bibliometric methods: article title, first author, year of publication, journal of publication, total citations, country, institution, and keywords.

## Results:
Among the selected 100 articles, the most frequently cited one has 224 citations. The most commonly appearing journals, institutions, and countries are as follows: “Clinical Orthopaedics Related Research” (5 times); Fudan University, University of Texas System, and Royal Orthopaedic Hospital (4 times each), with China and the USA cited the most (21 times each). The year 2018 is the most productive year (17 articles). About 97 first authors contributed one article each, and 3 had 2 articles each. Among all 229 keywords, the top 3 in frequency are CHS (20%), recurrence (4%), and surgery (3%). Twenty article topics are related to surgical treatment.

## Conclusion:
Research on CHS recurrence is citation-rich but focuses more on treatments than understanding mechanisms, indicating a need for deeper mechanistic exploration for treatment breakthroughs in the future.

## 1. Introduction
Chondrosarcoma (CHS) is the second most common primary malignant bone tumor, accounting for 20% to 27% of all malignant bone tumors. Its incidence has been increasing over the years, and it has now become the most common malignant bone tumor in adults.[ , ] Due to the poor sensitivity of CHS to radiation therapy and chemotherapy, surgical treatment remains the primary approach for managing CHS.
Although many studies suggest that achieving satisfactory surgical margins can reduce the recurrence rate of CHS, in practical surgeries, the complex anatomical structures often result in high recurrence rates.[ – ] Currently, there is some evidence indicating that imaging omics can assist in the assessment of CHS recurrence,[ ] but the level of evidence is limited. Existing research mainly focuses on clinical studies related to CHS cases, the relationship between different treatment modalities and prognosis, and molecular biology studies related to the development of CHS.[ – ] Currently, there is no consensus on conservative treatment for CHS, and the underlying mechanisms of CHS recurrence have not been fully elucidated.
As research on CHS continues to advance, the number of related article reports is increasing. Researchers are spending more and more effort in conducting article searches. Citation analysis is a systematic method to identify the impact of scientific research, where articles with higher citation counts have a greater influence in the field. It is based on bibliometric methods and can help researchers quickly grasp the research focus and trends in a particular field.[ , ] However, there is currently no bibliometric analysis specifically focused on CHS, let alone a more precise exploration of CHS recurrence.
In this study, we retrieved the top 100 most-cited articles in the field of CHS recurrence. We conducted in-depth analyses of these articles with respect to their publication year, journals, authors, institutions, countries, keywords, and citations. We employed data visualization methods to illustrate the current research trends and directions in the CHS recurrence field. The primary objective of this study is to identify and analyze the 100 most-cited articles related to CHS recurrence, facilitating medical researchers in promptly accessing the research dynamics in this field.

## 2. Methods
This study conducted an article search in the Core Collection database of Web of Science (WOS) to retrieve all relevant publications on chondrosarcoma recurrence. The top 100 most-cited publications were identified. The following search criteria were used: “chondrosarcoma” limited to the “Title” field, “recurrence OR recur OR relapse” limited to the “Topic” field, language restricted to “English,” document types limited to “Article OR Review,” and publication date limited to “2013-01-01 to 2022-12-31” (spanning 10 years).
Using the above search criteria, a total of 305 article records were retrieved. Two independent reviewers conducted the selection process, resolved disagreements through discussion, and excluded records that were not relevant to CHS recurrence or for which complete article data could not be obtained. Finally, 100 of the most frequently cited articles and reviews were collected. The following information was recorded and analyzed: article title, first author, year of publication, journal, total number of citations, average annual citations, country, institution, and keywords. VOSviewer was used to construct the distribution of each important element.
By carefully reading the research methods in the collected articles, we classified the collected articles into 3 categories based on their research designs: review articles, clinical data-related studies (research related to diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis based on clinical information), and basic research (studies related to mechanisms at the DNA, RNA, protein, cellular, or animal levels). The articles were further categorized into 7 topics: surgical treatment-related studies, nonsurgical treatment-related studies, comprehensive treatment-related studies, diagnostic technique and characteristic analysis of CHS, analysis of prognostic factors, exploration of mechanisms underlying CHS occurrence and development, and case reports.

## 3. Results
According to our search command, we retrieved a total of 305 article records and selected 100 articles that were most frequently cited and relevant to CHS recurrence based on our inclusion criteria. The articles in the list were cited a total of 2438 times, with the highest citation count of 224 and averaging 24.38 citations. The article with the highest annual average citation count was a review article, cited 56 times per year, published in 2018 (Table ; Table 1, Supplemental Digital Content, http://links.lww.com/MD/L34). The year 2018 had the highest number of publications, with 17 articles, followed by 2014 with 16 articles (Fig. ). These articles were published in a total of 69 different journals, with the highest number of publications in “Clinical Orthopaedics Related Research” (5 articles), followed by “Annals of Surgical Oncology” (4 articles) (Fig. ). Among the top 100 articles, there were 97 different first authors, with 3 first authors appearing twice in the list: Fromm, J; Li, GS; Song, KH (Fig. ). Gronchi, Alessandro’s research on extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma is extensive, covering various aspects including mechanisms, diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis. The first authors in the list came from 72 different institutions, with 16 institutions appearing multiple times. The institutions with the highest frequency were Fudan University, China; University of Texas System, USA; and Royal Orthopaedic Hospital, UK (4 times each), followed by University of Munich, Germany, and University of Groningen, Netherlands (3 times each) (Fig. ). The coauthorship network of institutions is shown in Figure . These institutions were from 18 different countries and regions, with 12 countries and regions appearing multiple times. China and the USA had the highest frequency of appearances (21 times each), followed by UK (10 times), and Italy, Japan, and the Netherlands appeared 7 times each (Fig. ). A total of 229 keywords were obtained, with 185 keywords appearing only once, accounting for 81%. When the threshold was set to 2, we analyzed 44 keywords for cooccurrence overlay visualization (Fig. ). We also conducted cocitation analysis on all references of the first 100 articles, using a threshold of 7, and the top 100 most-cited articles included 46 references (Fig. ).  Based on our classification of the articles by study design, there were a total of 64 studies based on clinical data, 17 studies on basic research, and 19 review articles. When we categorized the articles based on their main themes, we found that there were 41 articles related to treatment, including 20 articles on surgical treatment, 8 articles on nonsurgical treatment, and 13 articles on a combination of surgical and nonsurgical treatments. There were 24 articles analyzing prognostic factors of CHS, 15 articles on the occurrence and recurrence mechanisms of CHS, 14 articles on the diagnostic technique and characteristic analysis of CHS, and 6 case report studies.
Among the 100 selected articles in our screening, the most highly cited publication was titled “Osteosarcoma, chondrosarcoma, and chordoma.” It was a review article published in 2018 by JS Whelan from University College London Hospitals NHS Foundation Trust in England. The article focused on treatment advancements in CHS, emphasizing that surgical resection remains the cornerstone of CHS treatment. However, the application of new biologic insights to therapeutic development indicates that improved treatments may soon be routine for patients with CHS for whom surgery alone is inadequate.[ ] The second most highly cited publication was titled “Mesenchymal CHS of bone and soft tissue: a systematic review of 107 patients in the past 20 years.” It was a systematic review study published in 2015 by Jie Xu from Peking University in China. The author similarly emphasized the necessity of surgical resection, stating that appropriate adjuvant therapy may reduce local recurrence but cannot benefit overall survival.[ ] The third most highly cited publication was titled “Mesenchymal CHS: prognostic factors and outcome in 113 patients. A European Musculoskeletal Oncology Society study.” It was a retrospective study published in 2015 by AM Frezza from RLUK-Research Libraries UK in England. The author affirmed the dominant role of surgery and highlighted the effectiveness of adjuvant chemotherapy for localized disease. The study also found that tumor metastasis had the greatest impact on survival.[ ]
The most recent article in the list is titled “Is chemotherapy associated with improved overall survival in patients with dedifferentiated CHS? A SEER database analysis.” The first author is LD Cranmer, and the conclusion of the study is that chemotherapy is not associated with improved overall survival in patients with dedifferentiated CHS.[ ] On the other hand, the earliest article in the list is titled “CHS of the subglottic larynx: submucosal microdissection with the operating microscope.” The first author is Ryan S. Jackson, and the conclusion of the study is that conservative surgical resection is the primary treatment approach for laryngeal CHS.[ ]

## 4. Discussion
In recent years, the incidence of CHS has been increasing, making it the most common malignant bone tumor in adults. With the advancement of research and the development of related diagnostic and therapeutic techniques, there has been a growing number of article reports on CHS, promoting the development of the field of CHS research. However, the increasing volume of articles has also resulted in researchers spending more time searching for high-quality articles. Bibliometric analysis or citation analysis helps researchers quickly obtain high-quality articles information by summarizing and quantifying the scientific impact of relevant articles.[ ]
Our study found that the research focus on CHS primarily revolves around its treatment. Among the articles in the list, there are 41 that are related to the treatment of CHS, with surgical treatment accounting for 48.8% (20 articles) of the treatment-related articles. This indicates that due to the poor sensitivity of CHS to nonsurgical treatments such as radiotherapy, chemotherapy, and targeted therapy, most researchers are still dedicated to exploring improvements in surgical methods to control the recurrence of CHS. According to clinical studies, for low-grade CHS in the limbs, surgical treatment generally yields good prognosis, with local recurrence rates ranging from 0% to 7.7%.[ ] For CHS occurring in the pelvis/sacrum, studies have shown recurrence rates of approximately 18% to 45%.[ – ] As for CHS occurring in the complex anatomical location of the spine, only with en bloc resection can the recurrence rate be controlled at 3% to 8%. However, if en bloc resection is not performed or the margins are unsatisfactory, the recurrence rate can be as high as 80%.[ ]
Due to the lack of breakthroughs in surgical treatment for CHS, 31.7% (13 articles) of researchers have shifted their focus to combined surgical and nonsurgical treatments, aiming to control the progression and postoperative recurrence of CHS through adjuvant therapy. Additionally, 19.5% (8 articles) of researchers have concentrated on nonsurgical treatment studies, investigating the use of radiation therapy, chemotherapy, and targeted therapy for CHS treatment. Furthermore, 58.5% (24 articles) of researchers have conducted comprehensive analyses of various factors possibly related to prognosis, including clinical features and treatment approaches for CHS, with the intention of obtaining valuable information to guide CHS treatment and control its progression and recurrence. In order to gain a deeper understanding of the mechanisms underlying the occurrence and recurrence of CHS, 36.6% (15 articles) of researchers have explored CHS-related mechanisms at the genetic and molecular levels, aiming to elucidate the mechanisms of CHS development and overcome the limitations of CHS treatment.[ – , , ]
Through an in-depth analysis of the top 5 most-cited articles, which we conducted, we found that the focus of these 5 articles remains on exploring various treatment modalities. While acknowledging the effectiveness of surgical treatment, they advocate for adjuvant therapy to prevent and control CHS recurrence. Although they agree that adjuvant therapy can effectively reduce CHS recurrence rates, they present different conclusions regarding whether adjuvant therapy can improve patients’ overall survival. They also highlight the limitations of existing treatment guidelines and the limited efficacy of various treatment approaches, indicating the need to explore new therapeutic methods for CHS treatment.
To account for the potential influence of publication year on the overall citation count, we conducted an analysis of the annual average citation count for the articles in the list. We found that the annual average citation count for the top 10 articles was all above 12. The article with the highest annual average citation count was also the most-cited article overall. Among the top 10 articles with the highest annual average citation count, 6 were clinical studies, while 2 were review articles and 2 were basic research studies. Their research topics were diverse, covering almost all categories, with the most prominent being articles analyzing prognostic factors, accounting for 40% (4 articles) of the top 10.
Our analysis revealed that the journal “Clinical Orthopaedics and Related Research” had the highest number of publications (5 of 100), with 4 of them published in the past 5 years. This peer-reviewed orthopedic journal is the official publication of the Association of Bone and Joint Surgeons, providing new and important orthopedic knowledge for researchers in related fields. It covers various aspects of clinical research related to orthopedics, including diagnosis, treatment, and prognosis. Since the treatment of CHS is an important clinical issue, it is not surprising that articles from this journal frequently appear in the list.
Citation analysis, using bibliometric methods, visually demonstrates the impact of articles and allows researchers to quickly identify research hotspots in their respective fields. However, this study has limitations. First, although WOS is a comprehensive global database widely used for citation analysis, there may still be differences in citation indexing with other databases. Second, our study was limited to articles published in English, which may have resulted in the omission of relevant studies in other languages. Future studies should take these factors into consideration.

## 5. Conclusion
In this study, we selected the top 100 most-cited articles related to CHS recurrence from the Core Collection database of WOS. By analyzing the topics, publication journals, keywords, first authors, institutions, countries, total citations, and annual average citations of these articles, the current research hotspots in the field of CHS recurrence were identified, primarily focusing on the improvement and breakthroughs in treatment approaches. This study provides a valuable resource for researchers to quickly access high-quality articles in the field of CHS recurrence, offering insights for future research and clinical practice in this area.
