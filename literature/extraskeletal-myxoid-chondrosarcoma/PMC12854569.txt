Soft tissue chondroma may arise from the synovial sheath of the long tendons, paratendinous soft tissues, or para-articular tissues. It is thought to stem either from pluripotent cells within the tenosynovium or through metaplasia within the tendon sheath.
Radiologically, soft tissue chondroma shows a well-demarcated soft tissue lesion. Irregular, ring-like, or curvilinear calcification is often described. Compression deformities or erosions of the adjacent bone are not uncommon.
Soft tissue chondroma can sometimes show extensive myxoid changes, which can cause a diagnostic challenge. Here, we present a case of subcutaneous soft tissue chondroma with myxoid changes.
A 47-year-old female presented with a slowly growing, painless swelling on the left thumb for the past 5 years. Locally, there was a hard, fixed, lobulated, nontender subcutaneous swelling on the dorsal aspect of the left thumb, measuring 4 cm × 4 cm with well-defined borders. It was not attached to the overlying skin.
X-ray of the hand showed a large soft tissue mass around the phalanges [ ]. The mass had clusters of curvilinear and punctate calcifications. It caused scalloping and pressure erosions of the volar cortex of the distal phalanx of the thumb, with remodeling of the volar concavity of the proximal phalanx volar cortex. Coronal Proton density-weighted fat-saturated magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) [ ] showed a lobulated homogenous, significantly hyperintense soft tissue mass measuring 4.5 × 3.8 × 2.5 cm. The epicenter of the mass was in the soft tissue, causing pressure erosions and remodeling of the phalangeal cortices. The clinical and radiological impression was of a giant cell tumor of the soft part.
 The tumor was excised in-toto. On gross examination, it was a 6 × 3.8 × 3 cm, well-encapsulated, lobulated soft tissue swelling in the subcutaneous plane, also extending into the dermis. Histopathological examination revealed unremarkable epidermis. An encapsulated, lobulated tumor was identified in the dermis and subcutaneous plane, composed of lobules of mature hyaline cartilage with peripheral calcification [ ]. The chondrocytes showed minimal atypia. Some of the lobules were cellular, composed of small to medium-sized cells, embedded in a myxoid background [ ]. The cells had round nuclei, bland chromatin, and a scant to moderate amount of eosinophilic cytoplasm. There was no nuclear atypia, mitosis, or necrosis. S100 immunostaining highlighted the chondrocytes. The smaller cells were negative for S100, keratin, desmin, CD34, ERG, CD99, smooth muscle actin, CD138, p63, and calponin. Ki-67 proliferative index was low (1-2%). A diagnosis of soft tissue chondroma with myxoid changes was made. Fluorescent in situ hybridization (EWSR1 translocation dual color probe, Abbott) did not reveal rearrangement of the EWSR1 gene.
 The patient was discharged in a stable condition. She did not receive any further treatment. There was no recurrence at 23 months follow-up.
Chondroma of soft parts show wide morphological variation, making its recognition difficult at times. Most cases show lobules of mature hyaline cartilage with variable calcification. Fibrosis and ossification may be seen. Myxoid changes are sometimes encountered.[ ] Our case showed significant myxoid changes. Thus, soft tissue chondroma with myxoid changes needs to be differentiated from its close mimickers, mainly extraskeletal myxoid chondrosarcoma (EMC), cutaneous mixed tumor (chondroid syringoma) and acral fibrochondromyxoid tumor (AFCMT).
Clinically, EMC is a large tumor, that arises in the deep soft tissue of the proximal extremities and limb girdles showing round to oval cells with eosinophilic cytoplasm disposed in strands or cords in a predominantly myxoid stroma. The cartilaginous component is absent or inconspicuous. These tumors exhibit greater cellularity, cytological atypia, and increased mitosis.[ ] EMC is characterized by NR4A3 gene rearrangement, located on chromosome 9, with EWSR1 gene arrangement being the commonest translocation partner.[ ] Chondroid syringoma is a dermal tumor that shows tubular cystic structures lined by two layers of cells along with stroma which has a mucoid, faintly basophilic appearance.[ ] AFCMT shows vascularized septa separating lobules with tumor cells which are predominately epithelioid, plasmacytoid and spindled with reniform nuclei, embedded in fibromyxoid stroma. Hyaline cartilage is rarely observed. These tumor cells are ERG and CD34 positive and S100 negative. These cases show THBS1:ADGRF5 fusion.[ ] All these features are in contrast to soft tissue chondromas. Careful histological examination, along with clinical and radiological correlation can differentiate these lesions from soft tissue chondroma.
Surgery is the treatment of choice, but recurrences are not uncommon.[ ] The index case did not show recurrence at 23 months follow-up.
To conclude, soft-tissue chondromas are relatively uncommon cartilaginous tumors that arise from hyaline cartilage. It can present as a subcutaneous mass. Soft tissue chondroma with myxoid changes should be differentiated from other myxoid soft tissue tumors. Clinical, radiological, and histopathological correlation is important to accurately diagnose this entity.
