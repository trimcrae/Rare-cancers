## Radiotherapy-Triggered PROTAC
X-ray
radiotherapy is a first-line clinical treatment method for cancer. A). The radiation-responsive
cage can be cleaved by X-rays. When the tumor site is irradiated by
X-rays, RT-PRO undergoes a cleavage chemical reaction to release PRO,
thereby specifically targeting the BRD2/3/4 proteins of tumor cells.
MCF-7 cells were treated with varying concentrations of PRO, RT-RPO,
or RT-PRO, followed by X-ray radiation, and BRD4 levels were subsequently
observed. As determined by Western blotting, the DC50 of
the PRO group was 31.23 nM, and the D
max was 77.47%. The RT-PRO group failed to degrade BRD4 even at the
highest tested concentration. In cells cotreated with RT-PRO and X-ray
radiation, the DC50 was approximately 8.44 nM, and the D
max reached 68.75%. These results demonstrated
that the caging modification enhanced RT-PRO stability compared to
PRO, X-ray radiation enabled precise control of RT-PRO’s protein
degradation activity, and activated RT-PRO exhibited relatively high
BRD protein degradation efficiency in MCF-7 cells ( B). Subsequently, MCF-7 cells
were subcutaneously injected into the right flanks of BALB/c nude
mice, which were randomly divided into five groups. The results showed
that the tumor volume in the PBS group increased 8.89-fold. In contrast,
tumor volumes in the X-ray + RT-PRO and X-ray + PRO groups increased
1.89- and 1.63-fold, respectively, indicating that X-ray + RT-PRO
or X-ray + PRO treatment significantly inhibited tumor growth and
that radiotherapy and PROTACs achieved synergistic antitumor effects
( C).

## Immune-Regulated PROTAC
Cancer immunotherapy
represents a cutting-edge approach for treating malignant tumors,
which inhibits tumor progression by reactivating the intrinsic immune
system, particularly cytotoxic T lymphocytes and phagocytes. B). NPRO has strong permeability and retention
in tumors. Under 660 nm light irradiation, it can generate singlet
oxygen (1O2) to eliminate tumor cells. Caspase-3
is a key death protease located downstream of the apoptotic pathway
in mammalian cells. Cytoplasmic caspase-3 exists in an inactive zymogen
form. The appearance of apoptotic signals can cause caspase-3 to be
cleaved and activated. The activated caspase-3 further leads to amplification
of the protease cascade cleavage, ultimately causing the cell to die.
NPRO releases TAAs and induces ICD by enhancing the expression of
caspase-3 in tumor cells. Overexpressed caspase-3 further enhances
DEVD cleavage, triggering the release of PROTAC (aPRO). aPRO specifically
binds to SHP2 and UBR protein via the SP segment and UBR E3 ligase
receptor, respectively, inducing continuous SHP2 degradation through
the UPS. SHP2 depletion blocks immunosuppressive signals, including
CD47/SIRPα and PD-1/PD-L1 pathways,
 A). Additionally, combining cancer photoimmunotherapy with

effects via activatable photoimmunotherapy. Its small particle size
ensures efficient tumor-targeted accumulation, while biocompatibility

## Conclusions
In summary, this review summarizes
the synthesis and optimization
methods of PROTAC prodrugs based on targeted protein degradation.
Currently, the activation methods of PROTAC prodrugs under study mainly
include photostimulation activation, sonodynamic activation, enzymatic
catalysis, bioorthogonal reactions, immunomodulation, and ligand coupling.
In addition, to overcome the problems of high molecular weight and
low solubility of PROTAC prodrugs themselves, some delivery platforms
have also been developed, such as PROTAC prodrug nanoplatforms, albumin-binding
PROTAC, etc. Other treatment methods can also be used in combination
with PROTAC for synergistic cancer treatment. They can serve as activation
conditions for PROTAC prodrugs, such as immune responses, photosensitizers,
etc. By combining medical chemistry, materials science, nanomedicine,
etc., people can rationally customize stimulable-activated PROTAC
prodrugs for the precise degradation of proteins within tumor cells,
thereby facilitating the precise treatment of cancer.
