Dear Editor,

We present an effective target to treat colitis would be achieved through the transcriptional regulation and trans‐Golgi translocation of NR4A1, so as to inhibit NLR family, pyrin domain containing 3 (NLRP3) inflammasome. Inflammatory bowel disease (IBD) is a chronic and intractable digestive tract inflammatory disease that affects the millions of people; blocking immune responses and inflammatory cytokines have become effective strategies for treating colitis. NR4A1 (also known as TR3, NGFIB or Nur77), a member of the nuclear receptor NR4A family, exerts a protective role in colitis, , however, its effect and mechanism on pyroptosis or NLRP3 inflammasome is still unclear.
In current study, NR4A1 expression is significantly increased in colon tissues from C. rodentium (Citrobacter rodentium)‐induced mice colitis (Figure  ). And NR4A1 deficiency increases C. rodentium‐induced mortality, weight loss, colon shorten, colon mucosal damage and the colonization of C. rodentium (Figure  and Figure ). For pyroptosis and inflammasome, NR4A1 deficiency increases the level of lactate dehydrogenase (LDH), interleukin‐1β (IL‐1β), IL‐18, caspase‐1 p20 and the cleavage of gasdermin D (GSDMD) in serum or colon (Figure  and Figure ). These results indicated that NR4A1 deficiency exacerbates inflammasome activation and pyroptosis in vivo.
 To investigate the role of NR4A1 in inflammasome activation, we isolated bone marrow‐derived macrophages (BMDMs) from WT mice and NR4A1−/−
 mice. For canonical inflammasome, the results showed that NR4A1 only inhibits NLRP3 inflammasome, without affecting NLRC4 or AIM2 inflammasome (Figure ). NR4A1 expression is increased after the first and the second stimulation of NLRP3 inflammasome (Figure ). And NR4A1 deficiency augments Nigericin‐induced LDH, IL‐1β, IL‐18 and caspase‐1 cleavage (Figure  and Figure ). Except Nigericin, NLRP3 inflammasome is reported to be activated by other stimulants ; NR4A1 deficiency also significantly increases adenosine triphosphate (ATP) and monosod ium urate‐induced NLRP3 inflammasome activation (Figure ). For non‐canonical NLRP3 inflammasome activation, NR4A1 expression is increased after treatment, and NR4A1 deficiency increases cytosolic lipopolysaccharide (cLPS)‐induced LDH, IL‐1β and IL‐18 production (Figure ); WT and NR4A1−/−
 mice were injected with lipopolysaccharide (LPS) to induce non‐canonical NLRP3 inflammasome activation, NR4A1 deficiency exacerbates LPS‐induced jejunum pathological injury, concentration of IL‐1β and IL‐18 in serum (Figure ). During NLRP3 inflammasome activation, ASC and NLRP3 oligomerization are acritical steps for the subsequent caspase‐1 activation. , , , 
NR4A1 deficiency aggravates Nigericin‐induced amounts of ASC specks, and the interaction between NLRP3 and ASC in BMDMs (Figure  and Figure ). Meanwhile, overexpression of NR4A1 inhibits canonical and non‐canonical NLRP3 inflammasome activation in THP‐1 and BMDMs (Figure and Figure ). Taken together, these results provided sound evidence that NR4A1 would inhibit the activation and assembly of NLRP3 inflammasome.
 The activation of NLRP3 inflammasome requires two sequential steps, that is, priming and activation, and the key priming event is the NF‐κB pathway‐mediated transcription of pro‐IL‐1β and NLRP3. 
NR4A1 deficiency increases the expression of NLRP3 and pro‐IL‐1β in macrophages and colon tissues (Figure  , Figures and ). As a transcription factor, sequence analysis of the mouse NLRP3 and IL‐1β promoter reveals that either of which contains the conserved potential monomeric NR4A1 binding sites NGFIB response element (NBREs) (Table ). Through luciferase reporter assays, promoter deletion and deletion experiments, the results showed that NR4A1 inhibited IL‐1β and NLRP3 promoter activity (Figure  and Figure ). Accordingly, the ChIP assay results showed that NR4A1 could bind to the promoter of IL‐1β and NLRP3 (Figure  and Figure ). These results importantly indicated NR4A1 transcriptionally inhibits NLRP3 and IL‐1β independent of NF‐κB.
 Increasing evidence supports that NR4A1 function is also controlled at the post‐transcriptional level, predominantly via interaction, modification and subcellular localization. , We reconstructed NLRP3 inflammasome in HEK293T cells to eliminate transcriptional regulation, and overexpression of NR4A1 significantly decreases Nigericin‐induced NLRP3 inflammasome activation (Figure ). Co‐immunoprecipitation showed that NR4A1 interacts with NLRP3 in macrophages (Figure  and Figure ). The association between NR4A1 and NLRP3 is substantially increased by LPS, Nigericin or ATP (Figure ). Through transfecting GFP‐NR4A1 and Flag‐NLRP3 in HEK293T cells, we found the interaction between NR4A1 and NLRP3 is enhanced by Nigericin or ATP, independently of the first signal (Figure ). To clarify the key domain of the interaction, the results showed that NR4A1 interacts with the NACHT domain of NLRP3, and NLRP3 interacts with the LBD domain of NR4A1 (Figure ). For NLRP3‐ASC complex formation, after the LBD structure is deleted, the inhibitory effect of NR4A1 on NLRP3 specks is disappeared (Figure ). These results suggested that the LBD domain is the key domain for NR4A1 to inhibit NLRP3 inflammasome.
Interestingly, under the stimulation of Nigericin or ATP, NR4A1 and NLRP3 aggregate into specks and co‐localized together (Figure ). Usually, once NLRP3 inflammasome activation, NLRP3 migrates from the endoplasmic reticulum to trans‐Golgi or mitochondria. , We labeled the trans‐Golgi with TGN38, and NR4A1 co‐localizes with trans‐Golgi, after activating NLRP3 inflammasome (Figure ). NR4A1 is activated by 6‐MP (Mercaptopurine) and Csn‐B (Cyclosporine‐B), which promotes NR4A1 aggregating into specks and co‐localize in the trans‐Golgi (Figure  and Figure ). Interestingly, 6‐MP or Csn‐B promotes the interaction between NLRP3 and NR4A1 and decreases the interaction between NLRP3 and ASC (Figure  ). For NLRP3 inflammasome, 6‐MP or Csn‐B suppresses ASC oligomerization, caspase‐1cleavage, IL‐1β and IL‐18 production (Figure ). These results indicated that the agonist of NR4A1 promotes the migration of NR4A1 to trans‐Golgi to suppress NLRP3 inflammasome activation. We also confirmed the effect of NR4A1 agonists in vivo, these results showed that 6‐MP or Csn‐B, inhibit C. rodentium‐induced colitis and NLRP3 inflammasome activation (Figure ). However, 6‐MP or Csn‐B could not inhibit Nigericin‐induced NLRP3 inflammasome activation in NR4A1−/‐
 BMDM (Figure  ).
In summary, NR4A1 exerts a protective effect against C. rodentium‐induced colitis through inhibiting NLRP3 inflammasome. Mechanistically, NR4A1 not only transcriptionally inhibits the expression of NLRP3 and IL‐1β, but also interacts with NLRP3 and jointly migrates to trans‐Golgi, blocking NLRP3 activation and assembly (Figure  ). Our findings offer a new idea for the treatment of IBD, which is, targeting transcriptional regulation and trans‐Golgi translocation of NR4A1.
