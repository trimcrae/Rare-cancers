## Abstract
Transcription cofactor p300 is characterized by histone acetyltransferase (HAT) activity, which endows p300 with dual functions of chromatin remodeling and gene transcription and participates in regulating various basic cellular functions. P300 mainly enhances the expression of downstream pro-inflammatory factors such as TNF-α and IL-6 by acetylating core transcription factors in the inflammatory signaling pathway, such as NF-κB and JAK/STAT. P300 also reshapes the epigenetic profile of important cellular components in inflammation, such as macrophages or T cells, by mediating histone H3K27ac modification. Based on the above molecular mechanisms, p300 plays a key role in various inflammatory diseases, including respiratory inflammatory, autoimmune, metabolic-related and neuroinflammatory diseases as well as infectious inflammation. Small molecule inhibitors targeting p300 show anti-inflammatory potential in various preclinical animal models; however, their tissue-specific targeting mechanisms and long-term safety need further exploration. The current review summarizes the molecular mechanism basis, cell specific functions, and targeted intervention strategies of p300 in inflammatory diseases, and explores the development of novel p300-based anti-inflammatory medication.
Keywords: P300, Inflammation, NF-κB, HIF-1α, Macrophage; T cell

## Introduction
P300 was initially discovered while studying the interaction between adenovirus E1A proteins and was named after its molecular weight of approximately 300 kDa. P300 belongs to the CREB-binding protein (CBP) family and has high homology and functional overlap with CBP, collectively referred to as p300/CBP [ ]. Epigenetic regulation refers to a set of molecular mechanisms that modulate gene expression through chemical modifications (such as DNA methylation, histone modifications, etc.) without altering the DNA sequence. The core direct function of p300 is to act as a histone acetyltransferase (HAT) [ ], catalyzing the acetylation of various histone lysine residues and other short chain acylation modifications (including histone crotonylation, histone propionylation/butyrylation, etc.), thereby serving as one of the main "writers" of the epigenetic landscape and dominating gene activation.
P300 contains multiple functional domains, including the N-terminal transcriptional activation domain, central HAT domain, and C-terminal protein interaction domains. These domains enable p300 to interact with various transcription factors (such as nuclear factor (NF)-κB, signal transducers and activators of transcription (STATs), and hypoxia related transcription factor (HIF)−1α) and basic transcription machinery, thereby regulating multiple signaling pathways [ ]. The HAT activity of p300 not only modifies histones (such as H3K27ac) and acetylates various transcription factors (such as p65 and p53), affecting their stability, nuclear localization, and transcriptional activity [ ].
P300 plays a central role in the inflammatory response. For example, p300 enhances DNA binding ability and transcriptional activity by acetylating the p65 subunit of NF-κB (such as K310 site), promoting the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines such as interleukin (IL)−6 and tumor necrosis factor (TNF)-α. In addition, p300 is involved in regulating Th17 cell differentiation, macrophage polarization, and inflammasome activation, and is closely related to the pathogenesis of various inflammatory diseases [ – ]. Herein, we review the specific role and related molecular mechanisms of p300 in inflammatory diseases, progress in the development of p300 as a drug target, and the outlook on future research directions regarding p300.

## EP300-Related Signaling Pathways in Inflammation
P300 is widely involved in regulating inflammatory responses through interactions with other key proteins and signaling pathways (Fig.  ).
 

## NF-κB Pathway
NF-κB is a core transcription factor in inflammatory response, and p300 enhances its transcriptional activity by acetylating key protein p65 subunits (such as K221 and K310 sites) in NF-κB signaling pathway. In a variety of inflammatory diseases, such as diabetes nephropathy and acute respiratory distress syndrome (ARDS), p300 forms a complex with p65 to promote inflammatory factor expression (IL-6 and TNF-α), thereby aggravating inflammation. In brain astrocytes, activating the adhesion molecule intercellular adhesion molecule-1 (ICAM-1), via the pro-inflammatory cytokine TNF-α, is regulated by the binding of the phosphorylated p65 subunit of NF-κB to p300 [ ]. Under the stimulation of pro-inflammatory signals TNF-α and lipopolysaccharides (LPS), PARP-1 and p300 synergistically activate NF-κB dependent gene expression in fibroblast. Poly(ADP-ribose)polymerase1 (PARP-1) directly interacts with p300 and enhances the interaction between NF-κB1/p50 and p300, jointly promoting inflammation [ ]. The interaction between p300, NF-κB, and bromodomain containing 4 (BRD4) in endothelial cells increases C–C motif chemokine ligand 2 (CCL2) expression, leading to macrophage accumulation and inflammation exacerbation, and portal hypertension [ ].

## JAK/STAT Pathway
P300 directly binds to STAT3 and enhances its transcriptional activity by acetylating the Lys685 site of STAT3, promoting downstream target gene expression in human hepatocyte cancer cell line (HepG2) [ ]. The amino-terminal region of STAT1 interacts with the CREB-binding domain of CBP/p300, whereas the carboxyl-terminal region of STAT1 interacts with the CBP/p300 domain of the adenovirus E1A protein. These interactions play important roles in interferon (IFN)-γ-induced transcription and anti-viral activity in human sarcoma cell line [ ]. Prostacyclin inhibits STAT1 activation via several mechanisms, one of which is STAT1-S727 phosphorylation in the transactivation domain, thereby reducing CBP/p300 recruitment to STAT1 and monocyte chemoattractant protein-1 (MCP-1), IL-8, regulated upon activation, normal T-cell expressed and secreted (RANTES), and TNF production inhibition induced by IFN-γ and IL-6 in monocytes [ ].

## HIF-1α Pathway
A two hybrid system has been used to detect the interaction between p300 and other transcription factors in vivo, HIF-1 α has been identified as a key transcription factor that directly interacts with p300 in human fetal osteoblast cells [ ]. Under hypoxic conditions, p300 binds to HIF-1α and activates the expression of target genes, such as vascular endothelial growth factor (VEGF). However, inflammatory signals, such as TNF-α, can competitively bind to p300 to inhibit the function of HIF-1α, thereby affecting HIF-1α-mediated tissue repair [ ]. In addition, NF-κB mediated inflammatory signaling can block the transactivation of HIF-1α at the HRE encoded gene by directly competing for p300 binding at the promoter site in osteoblast [ ].

## Other Key Proteins Interacting With P300 in Inflammatory Conditions

## Nuclear Factor Erythroid 2-related Factor 2 (NRF2)
NRF2 is the key transcription factor responsible for antioxidant response element (ARE)-dependent transcription. P300 enhances the promoter-specific DNA binding of NRF2 through acetylation and establishes acetylation as a novel mechanism that works synergistically with Kelch-like ECH-associated protein 1 (Keap1) mediated ubiquitination to regulate NRF2 dependent antioxidant responses in HEK293T cells [ ]. P300 acetyltransferase activity is essential for promoting NRF2 nuclear accumulation, stability, and protein expression in HEK293T cells [ ]. At the level of molecular mechanisms, the Neh4 and Neh5 domains of NRF2 can bind to the TAZ1 and TAZ2 domains of CBP/p300 with micromolar affinities in oxidative stress response [ ]. Sodium butyrate (NaB) inhibits histone deacetylase (HDAC) activity and increases the occupancy of the transcription factor aromatic hydrocarbon receptor (AHR) and co-activator p300 on the NRF2 promoter. In addition, the p300 inhibitor, C646, eliminates the effects of NaB in endothelial cells [ ].

## CCAAT Enhancer Binding Protein β (C/EBPβ)
Under thromboxane induced IL-8 expression conditions, p300 forms a complex with C/EBPβ, promoting production of inflammatory factors in human lung epithelial cells [ ]. C/EBPβ is also a key component of NF-κB-mediated inflammation in human airway smooth muscle cells, affecting transcription through interaction with p300. Inhibition of the p300-C/EBPβ interaction reduces the acetylation level of p65 at the lysine 310 site, thereby weakening p65 transcriptional activity in human airway smooth muscle cells [ ].

## RUNT-related Transcription Factor 2 (Runx2)
Under chronic inflammatory conditions, p300 and Runx2 synergistically activate a key downstream target, a disintegrin and metalloproteinase with thrombospondin motif (ADAMTS), leading to extracellular matrix degradation [ ]. Carboxyl-terminal binding proteins 2 (CtBP2) can form a complex with p300 and the transcription factor Runx2. Low nicotinamide adenine dinucleotide-hydrogen (NADH) levels in atrophic non-connected bone tissue disrupt CtBP2 dimerization and enhance the accessibility blockade of bone-related target gene promoters via the p300-Runx2 complex [ ].

## Jun Proto-Oncogene (C-Jun)
C-Jun and p300 at CRE/E-box sites are the molecular foundations of epidermal growth factor (EGF)-induced Cyclooxygenase-2 (COX-2) gene transcription. P300 overexpression enhances COX-2 promoter activity in human epidermoid carcinoma A431 cells overexpressing c-Jun or treated with EGF [ ]. A 12 amino acid region in c-Jun DNA-binding domain is is necessary for Adenovirus Early Region 1 A (E1A) transcriptional repression and is acetylated by p300 [ ].

## Epithelial Specific Antigen 1 (ESE-1)
Co-transfection with p300 and epithelium-specific ETS-1 (ESE-1) enhances the transcriptional activity of ESE-1. The induction of the inflammatory cytokine IL-1 by ESE-1 is associated with a parallel increase in p300 expression in vascular endothelial cells, indicating that the transcriptional activity of ESE-1 is positively regulated by its interaction with p300 in an inflammatory environment [ ]. The Ku protein family interacts with P300 to modulate ESE-1 in the kidney tissues of diabetic mice and hyperglycemic human renal glomerular microvascular endothelial cells (HRGECs) [ ].

## Orphan Nuclear Receptor 77 (Nur77)
Nur77 is an orphan member of the nuclear hormone receptor superfamily, playing an essential role in controlling cellular basic functions such as cell proliferation, apoptosis and glucose metabolism. Acetylation of Nur77 is regulated by p300 and HDAC1 in human and rat cancer cell lines, indicating that acetylation is an important post-translational modification for rapid Nur77 turnover [ ].

## Interferon Regulatory Factor 3 (IRF3)
Transcription factor dimerization enables the trans-autoacetylation of p300 in a highly conserved and intrinsically disordered autoinhibitory lysine-rich loop, resulting in p300 activation [ ]. Virus infection induces phosphorylation of IRF3 on serine residues at positions 385 and 386, thereby allowing it to complex with the co-activator CBP/p300 with nuclear translocation [ , ]. Recently, a non-canonical role for IRF3 as an upstream activator of p300 and establish the IRF3-p300 axis as an essential signaling pathway that ensures proper mitotic progression [ ].

## The Relationship between p300 and Inflammatory Diseases
In recent years, a series of literature reports have shown that p300 plays an important role in various inflammatory diseases, including respiratory inflammatory diseases, metabolic inflammatory-related diseases, autoimmune diseases, neuroinflammatory diseases and infectious inflammation (Fig.  ).
 

## Inflammatory Diseases of the Respiratory System

## Acute Respiratory Distress Syndrome (ARDS)
The causes of ARDS are diverse, but the core is the uncontrolled inflammatory response. Both the direct damage to the lungs (such as pneumonia, inhalation) or serious diseases in other parts of the body (such as sepsis, pancreatitis) will ultimately activate inflammatory cells such as neutrophils and macrophages, induce “cytokine storms” such as TNF-α, IL-1β, IL-6 and IL-17, and destroy the blood-air barrier in the lungs, leading to pulmonary edema and respiratory failure [ ]. The mRNA levels of p300, CBP, and retinoic acid receptor-related orphan receptor γt (RORγt), as well as the IL-6 and IL-17 concentrations in the plasma are substantially higher in patients with ARDS than those in the control group. In addition, the expression levels of p300, CBP, and IL-6 are higher in the non-survivor group than those in the survivor group. P300 expression is positively related to the RORγt and IL-17 levels, as well as and disease prognosis of ARDS [ ]. In addition, High p300 and RORγt expression is also observed in lung tissue of acute lung injury (ALI) mice; p300 inhibitors can alleviate lung tissue inflammation and lung injury scores by reducing RORγt levels and IL-17 secretion in lung tissue of ALI mice [ ]. The monocyte-derived exon, CLMAT3, activates the proinflammatory signaling pathway CtBP2-p300-NF-κB complex and induces pro-inflammatory cytokines in ALI mice [ ]. Hsa-miR-7-5p is upregulated in the biopsies of patients with ARDS. The elevated level of hsa-miR-7-5p mediated by CtBP1-p300-AP1 complex can target ATXN1, thereby triggering NF-κB-dependent inflammatory response. Clinically, elevated p300/CBP expression is a potential single risk indicator for mortality in patients with ARDS, and interfering with this signaling pathway and blocking the inflammatory response may be a strategy for treating ARDS [ ].

## Asthma
The inflammatory triggers of asthma are widely present in our living environment, and its core is chronic allergic inflammation mediated by type 2 immune response [ ]. P300 promotes airway inflammation and remodeling by acetylating the ORMDL3 promoter region. Inhibiting the HAT activity of p300 can alleviate airway hyperresponsiveness and fibrosis [ ]. In human tracheal smooth muscle cells (HTSMCs), cigarette smoke extract (CSE) induces COX-2 dependent Prostaglandin E2 (PGE2) production through the PKC/c-Src/EGFR and PDGFR/PI3K/Akt signaling pathways, leading to the recruitment of p300 and NF-κB complexes, which links CSE with asthma [ ]. The activation of mitogen-activated protein kinase (MAPK), NF-κB, and p300 is necessary for IL-1-induced cytoplasmic phospholipase A2 (cPLA2) expression and PGE2 secretion in HTSMCs [ ].
Adiponectin activates the PKC/NADPH oxidase/mitochondrial pathway via AdipoR1/2-dependent activation, leading to reactive oxygen species (ROS) accumulation, p300 phosphorylation, and histone H4 acetylation in human alveolar type II cells, thereby stimulating the expression of cPLA-2 and COX-2 in human alveolar Type II Cells [ ]. Upregulation of intercellular adhesion molecule-1 (ICAM-1) is often associated with lung inflammation and disease. Thrombin induces ICAM-1 expression in human alveolar epithelial cells (HPAEpiCs) through c-Src/PDGFR/PI3K/Akt dependent NF-κB/p300 activation [ ]. Vascular cell adhesion molecule-1 (VCAM-1) expression is strongly associated with pulmonary inflammatory diseases. Endothelin-1 enhances VCAM-1 expression via the MAPK/Elk-1/p300, endothelin receptor/Src/MPP/EGFR, and PDGFR/PI3K/AKT/p42/p44 pathways in HTSMCs, promoting the occurrence and amplification of pathological airway inflammation [ ].

## Metabolic Inflammatory-related Diseases

## Diabetes
Overnutrition and obesity lead to dysfunction of adipose tissue and imbalance of gut microbiota, resulting in the release of free fatty acids and endotoxins (LPS) into blood. These substances activate immune cells such as macrophages, producing pro-inflammatory factors TNF-α, IL-1 β, IL-6. They interfere with insulin signaling, causing insulin resistance. As compensation, pancreatic beta cells work under overload and ultimately fail and undergo apoptosis under toxic effects such as IL-1β. As a result, blood sugar is out of control and clinical diabetes occurs [ ]. IL-6 and TNF-α production and p300 expression in type 2 diabetes are up-regulated. p300 promotes the activation of NF-κB subunit p65 in type 2 diabetes through direct binding with p65, thereby promoting IL-6 and TNF-α production in diabetic mice [ ]. P300 and serine/threonine kinase salt induced kinase 2 (SIK2) are key upstream regulators of carbohydrate response element binding protein (ChREBP) activity. Knockout of liver-specific SIK2 and overexpression of p300 leads to hepatic steatosis, insulin resistance, and inflammation, and this phenotype is reversed by co-overexpression of SIK2 and p300. In a mouse model of type 2 diabetes, low SIK2 activity is associated with increased p300 HAT activity, excessive ChREBP acetylation, and liver steatosis. Inhibiting liver p300 activity may be beneficial for the treatment of liver steatosis in type 2 diabetes, and SIK2 activators and specific p300 inhibitors have been identified as potential targets for drug interventions [ ]. Sodium butyrate (NaB) inhibits HDAC activity and increases occupancy of aromatic hydrocarbon receptors (AHR) and p300 in NRF2 promoter. In addition, the p300 inhibitor, C646, eliminates the efficacy of NaB. P300 mediates the transcriptional activation of Nrf2 by NaB. Therefore, the p300-NRF2 axis is necessary for self-defense and NaB resistance in diabetes-induced aortic endothelial dysfunction [ ].

## Obesity
The main cause of inflammation in obesity can be seen as the dysfunction of adipose tissue itself and its interaction with other organs. The core lies in the fact that excessively swollen adipose tissue is no longer just an inert energy storage reservoir, but has become a highly active and disordered endocrine and immune organ, continuously outputting inflammatory signals [ ]. Leptin activates the MAPK/NF-κB/p300 cascade via OB-R dependent activation, promoting the expression of pulmonary cytoplasmic phospholipase A2-α [ ]. LPS activates the transcription factor X-box binding protein 1 (XBP1) through the ER stress sensor inositol requiring enzyme 1 (IRE1), thereby inducing p300. P300 subsequently acetylates insulin receptor substrates 1/2 (IRS1/2), inhibits its binding to insulin receptors, and disrupts insulin signaling. The pharmacological inhibition of p300 HAT activity using specific inhibitors C646 can improve insulin sensitivity and reduce hyperglycemia in obese mice [ ]. The increase in palmitate levels in patients with obesity creates an inflammatory environment which enhances IL-6 production through chromatin remodeling, making palmitate beneficial for metabolic inflammation. The initiating effect of palmitate is related to the enhanced binding of p300 and recruitment of transcription factors to the IL6 promoter region [ ]. Thus, p300 acetyltransferase activity may be a promising therapeutic target for the treatment of patients with obesity.

## Autoimmune Diseases (AID)

## Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
The inflammation of RA is mainly caused by autoimmune abnormalities. In genetically susceptible individuals, environmental factors such as smoking and infection may trigger immune system dysregulation, producing antibodies against their own joint tissues (such as anti-CCP antibodies). These antibodies activate immune cells (such as T cells and macrophages) and release large amounts of pro-inflammatory factors (TNF-α, IL-6, IL-17, etc.), driving chronic inflammation of synovial tissue, ultimately eroding cartilage and bones, leading to joint destruction [ ]. P300 promotes Th17 cell differentiation and IL-17 production through the STAT3-RORγt axis [ ]. Correspondingly, the selective CBP/Ep300 bromodomain inhibitor CBP30 can inhibit Th17 response [ ]. CBP and p300 play important roles in regulating stress-related gene expression in RA patient-derived synovial fibroblasts with enriched pathways associated with oxidative stress, hypoxia, autophagy, and proteasome function [ ]. A long non-coding RNA NEAT1 promotes IL-18 secretion through CBP/p300, exacerbating arthritis [ ]. The natural compound cryptotanshinone alleviates arthritis symptoms by inhibiting the HAT activity of p300 [ ]. iCBP112, a selective inhibitor of CBP/p300 that inhibits T cell activation and cytokine signaling pathways, reduces the expression of many pro-inflammatory cytokines, including IL-2, IFN-γ, and IL-17A. In addition, inhibiting CBP/p300 in T cells from the inflamed synovium of patients with juvenile idiopathic arthritis leads to decreased expression of inflammation-related genes [ ].

## Psoriasis (PSO)
The core inflammatory trigger of psoriasis is abnormal activation of the immune system. In the context of genetic susceptibility, factors such as infection, trauma, or stress can trigger innate and adaptive immune responses. Among them, dendritic cells are abnormally activated, driving T cells (especially Th17 cells) to differentiate and produce a large amount of key inflammatory factors such as IL-17, IL-23, and TNF-α. These cytokines work together to cause excessive proliferation of skin keratinocytes and infiltration of inflammatory cells, resulting in characteristic erythema and psoriasis plaques [ ]. P300 is abnormally activated in psoriatic fibroblasts and promotes fibronectin expression. The selective CBP/p300 inhibitor, A485, alleviates skin inflammation [ ]. In keratinocytes under inflammatory conditions, activation of AhR negatively regulates thymic stromal cytokine gene expression through the protein kinase Cδ(PKCδ)-p300-NF-κB pathway. In addition, AhR activation induces phosphorylation of p300 serine 89 site mediated by PKCδ, thereby reducing the acetylation and DNA binding activity of NF-κB p65 with thymic stromal lymphopoietin (TSLP) gene promoter [ ].

## Inflammatory Bowel Disease (IBD)
Genetic defects lead to impaired intestinal epithelial barrier function and immune regulation disorders, making it easier for gut microbiota to activate immune cells in the intestinal mucosa. These cells release large amounts of pro-inflammatory factors such as tumor necrosis factor and interleukin, causing chronic inflammation and ultimately leading to intestinal tissue damage and clinical symptoms [ ]. p-STAT1 and p300 promote the expression of TNF-α-inducible protein 2 (TNFAIP2) and lymphocyte cytosolic protein 2 (LCP2) by increasing the presence of H3K27ac on their enhancers and participate in the pathogenesis of IBD. In addition, p300 inhibitor C646 inhibits dextran sulfate sodium (DSS)-induced acute mice colitis [ ].

## Neuroinflammatory Diseases

## HIV-related Neurocognitive Disorder (HAND)
The inflammatory core cause of HIV related neurocognitive dysfunction lies in the sustained activation of immune cells and the neurotoxicity of viral proteins, establishing a sustained 'neuroinflammatory hotbed' [ ]. HAND is a neurodegenerative disease associated with persistent neuro-inflammation and subsequent neuronal damage. The interaction between HIV Tat protein and p300/PCAF inhibits the neurotrophic factor signaling pathway, leading to neuronal apoptosis [ ]. Negative factor (Nef) is involved in HAND pathogenesis by inducing lncRNA AK006025 expression in primary mouse astrocytes. AK006025 knockout inhibits CXCL9, CXCL10, and CXCL11 expression in astrocytes treated with Nef protein. Mechanically, AK006025 is enriched in the promoter regions of CXCL9, CXCL10, and CXCL11 by binding to CBP/p300 [ ].

## Alzheimer’s Disease (AD)
The key inflammatory trigger of Alzheimer's disease originates from abnormal protein aggregation in the brain. P300 upregulates Follistatin-like protein 1 (FSTL1) expression through the acetylation of Runx2, exacerbating neuronal damage and inflammatory responses in mice AD models [ ]. Ethyl pyruvate (EP) is an acetate ester that has neuroprotective effects under various neuropathological conditions, including AD. EP induces nuclear translocation of Nrf2, which binds to ARE and inhibits iNOS expression by depleting p300 from p65 [ ]. Chaperone mediated autophagy (CMA) inhibits the activation of microglia through the p300 related NF-κB signaling pathway. Specifically, degradation of CMA substrate p300 reduces p65 acetylation, thereby repressing the transcription of proinflammatory factors and the activation of NLR family pyrin domain containing 3 (NLRP3) inflammasome [ ].

## Experimental Autoimmune Encephalomyelitis (EAE)
Autoimmune encephalomyelitis is a classic animal model of multiple sclerosis, and its miain inflammatory trigger is the abnormal attack of the immune system on the myelin sheath components of the central nervous system. After being activated, pathogenic T cells cross the blood–brain barrier and enter the central nervous system. After recognizing myelin antigens, they recruit and activate inflammatory cells such as macrophages, releasing large amounts of tumor necrosis factor and pro-inflammatory factors such as interferon, which together lead to myelin loss, axonal damage, and neurological dysfunction [ ]. Knockdown of lcnRNA Gm13568 inhibits the expression of notch homolog protein 1 (Notch1), proliferation of astrocytes, phosphorylation of STAT3, and production of inflammatory cytokines and chemokines, such as IL-6 and TNF-α. In IL-9-activated astrocytes, Gm13568 binds to the transcription co-activator CBP/p300, which is enriched in the Notch1 promoter region. Inhibiting the expression of Gm13568 in astrocytes reduces inflammation and demyelination in experimental autoimmune encephalomyelitis (EAE) mice, thereby delaying the progression of EAE [ ].

## Other Inflammatory Diseases

## Vascular Inflammation
Multiple risk factors, such as hypertension, hyperlipidemia, and smoking, first damage the endothelium of blood vessels, leading to lipid (mainly LDL) infiltration and oxidation. Oxidized LDL will attract monocytes into the vascular endothelium, differentiate into macrophages and phagocytosis of lipids to form foam cells. These cells release proinflammatory factors such as TNF-α and IL-1β, further recruit more immune cells, and jointly build an atherosclerotic plaque full of inflammation [ ]. Phenotypic changes in vascular smooth muscle cells (SMCs) in response to injury or inflammation are important components of vascular diseases. P300 knockdown accelerates human SMC differentiation, whereas inhibition of p300 HAT activity reduces SMC differentiation efficiency [ ]. Adenosine monophosphate-activated protein kinase (AMPK) overexpression inhibits the HAT of p300 through phosphorylation of Ser89, which in turn reduces TNF activated p300 mediated acetylation of p65 on Lys221, and decreases the DNA binding activity of NF-κB by suppressing its recruitment to its target gene promoter, thereby inhibiting the occurrence of vascular inflammation, including atherosclerosis [ ]. Inhibition of neutrophil chemotaxis through p300 mediated downregulation of IL-8 alleviates vascular inflammatory damage after lung transplantation via ischemia–reperfusion [ ].
Estrogen may block the expression of cell adhesion molecules during vascular inflammation associated with atherosclerosis. Interaction between estrogen receptor (ER) and p65 in SMCs expressing ERα and ERβ after exposure to 17β-estradiol. ER and p65 do not directly bind but compete for a limited amount of p300. P300 overexpression reduces the inhibitory effect of ER on p65 dependent transcription and p65 on ER-dependent translation [ ]. HDAC maintain prostaglandin E synthase 1 (PTGES1) expression in vascular SMCs and promotes the generation of PGE2 by recruiting p300 [ ]. Ox-LDL leads to insufficient autophagy in macrophages, accompanied by the cytoplasmic accumulation of transcription factor EB and increased ROS production. Activated p300 promotes the combination of BRD4 and the promoter region of the inflammatory gene, thus promoting inflammation and atherosclerosis [ ].

## Nephritis
Nephritis begins with specific triggers (such as infection or autoimmune disorders) that disrupt the body's immune balance, produce abnormal antibodies, and form immune complexes. These complexes deposit in the kidneys, activate the complement system, and recruit inflammatory cells such as neutrophils and macrophages for infiltration. The latter releases a large number of inflammatory cytokines and chemokines, directly damaging key structures such as glomeruli and renal tubules, ultimately leading to clinical manifestations such as hematuria, proteinuria, edema, hypertension, and decreased renal function [ ]. The Ku protein family interacts with p300 to regulate Ese-1 expression in human glomerular microvascular endothelial cells (HRGEC), thereby contributing to hyperglycemia-induced endothelial cell inflammation [ ]. The mechanism of IL-6 and TGF-1 production in rat kidney tissue is related to the upregulation of p300 and its mediated C/EBP acetylation by C5b-9, as well as the IL-6 and TGF-1 activation by C/EBP [ ]. The peroxisome proliferator-activated receptor γ(PPAR-γ) agonist pioglitazone reduces p65 acetylation through the AMPK-SIRT1/p300 pathway, thereby preventing the NF-κB activation in cisplatin-induced nephrotoxicity [ ].
The activation of p300/CBP increases ROS production in diabetic kidneys, which is at least partly due to NADPH oxidase (NOX) upregulation, inflammatory reactions, and extracellular matrix protein production in diabetic kidneys [ ]. Apabutalone (a BRD4 inhibitor) inhibits polo-like kinase 1 (PLK1) expression in high-glucose cultured human tubular epithelial (HK-2) cells through p300 dependent H3K27 acetylation of the PLK1 promoter. Apabutalone can improve renal prognosis and fibrosis in patients with diabetic kidney disease. BRD4 regulates the p300/H3K27ac/PLK1 axis, leading to NLRP3 inflammasome activation and subsequent pyroptosis, inflammation, and fibrosis [ ].

## Infectious Inflammation
The fundamental cause of infectious inflammation is the invasion of pathogens. When microorganisms such as bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites break through the physical barriers of the body, their unique pathogen related molecular patterns, such as bacterial lipopolysaccharides and viral nucleic acids, are recognized by pattern recognition receptors of immune cells. This recognition process immediately triggers the inflammatory signaling pathway, activating immune cells and releasing pro-inflammatory factors, thereby triggering local or systemic inflammatory responses [ ]. During infection with African swine fever virus (ASFV) and stable expression of viral proteins in murine macrophage raw 264.7 cells, treatment with LPS and IFN-γ resulted in downregulation of NO production, iNOS expression, and iNOS promoter activity. P300 overexpression reverses the inhibition of basal and LPS-IFN-γ induced iNOS promoter activity mediated by A238L (an NF-κB and NFAT inhibitor) [ ]. lncRNA RUNX2-AS1 increases in biopsies of patients with septic nonunion (SN). The transcription factor c-MYC can assemble a transcription complex with its partner Max, p300, and nuclear receptor co activator 2 (NCOA2), which then binds to the RUNX2-AS1 promoter to reactivate its transcription. LPS exposure increases the enrichment of the NCOA2-p300-c-MYC/Max complex on the RUNX2-AS1 promoter and activated its expression, thereby downregulating the expression of downstream target genes of RUNX2-AS1. These results demonstrate that a new signaling pathway contributes to the pathology of SN [ ] (Table  ).
 

## P300 as an Anti-inflammatory Drug Target
Over the past few years, the focus of research on p300 has transitioned from basic research to the assessment of its potential as a target for drug development. In the field of inflammatory diseases, a series of synthetic and natural small molecule compounds targeting p300 have emerged (Fig.  and Table  ).
 

## Small Molecule Inhibitors

## C646
C646 is an effective inhibitor of p300. C646 reduces the expression of pro-inflammatory genes in mouse macrophages RAW264.7 and precision-cut lung slices by repressing activation of NF-κB signaling pathway. It has been found that starting from 7 μM and higher concentrations, C646 can inhibit the activity of histone deacetylase of p300 [ ]. C646 treatment reduces p300 expression and increases histone H3 acetylation, resulting in decreased serum liver injury markers and improved histological abnormalities in methionine choline-deficient diet-fed mice. C646 also reduces lipid accumulation by regulating the generation of new adipose tissue and inhibiting inflammation, including excessive pro-inflammatory cytokine production and macrophage infiltration. In addition, C646 alleviates liver fibrosis and myofibroblast accumulation. This protective effect is achieved by reducing the expression of p53 and Bax to inhibit apoptosis, reducing lipid peroxidation to inhibit iron deposition, and enhancing antioxidant defenses. C646 also mitigates endoplasmic reticulum stress by downregulating unfolded protein response signaling molecules [ ]. The pharmacological inhibition of EP300 by C646 improves LPS-induced ALI by regulating CXCL1 in M1 alveolar macrophages [ ]. These results highlight the potential of p300 as a therapeutic target for liver and lung-related inflammation.

## A-485
A highly effective p300/CBP inhibitor that protects against ALI by inhibiting macrophage inflammatory response [ ]. In a mouse model of piperonymus-induced psoriasis-like skin inflammation, systemic treatment with A-485 reduced skin inflammation, immune cell recruitment, and inflammatory cytokine production. The mechanism may be related to several genes such as extra-domain A fibronectin splicing variants of fibronectin and integrin α 4 (ITGA4) in psoriatic fibroblasts [ ].

## PNSC928
An effective CtBP2-p300 interaction inhibitor, PNSC928, was identified using AlphaScreen. PNSC928 treatment inhibits the formation of the of the CtBP2-p300-NF-κB complex, downregulates the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines, with no obvious cytotoxicity is observed in normal cells including mouse pulmonary alverolar epithelial cells (MPAEpiC), mouse macrophage-like cell line (RAW264.7), mouse hepatocyte cells (FL83B), mouse epithelial-like cells (TKPTS), and mouse cardiomyocyte cell line (HL-1). In vivo administration of PNSC928 reduces the expression of pro-inflammatory genes regulated by ctbp2 in ARDS mice, leading to alleviation of inflammation and lung injury, and ultimately improving the prognosis of ARDS [ ].

## Natural Compounds

## Cryptotanshinone
Cryptotanshinone is a quinoid diterpene compound isolated from the root of Salvia Miotiorrhiza bunge. Cryptotanshinone has been used to treat an experimental arthritis model by inhibiting p300 mediated STAT3 acetylation and reducing Th17 cell differentiation [ ].

## Tanshinone IIA
Tanshinone IIA is the most active diterpenoid quinine pigment in danshen, which is an traditional Chinese medicine. Sodium Tanshinone IIA inhibits the colocalization of NF-κB and p300, suppresses their binding, and reduces the monolayer permeability of human retinal pigment epithelium cells. Therefore, Sodium Tanshinone IIA might be used to treat diabetic retinopathy and prevent inflammation [ ].

## Quercetin
Quercetin, a polyphenolic bioflavonoid, possesses multiple pharmacological actions including anti-inflammation. Quercetin inhibits the HAT activity of p300, blocks core downstream target COX-2 and angiogenesis, and exerts anti-inflammatory effects in human endothelial cells [ ].

## Garcinol
Garcinol, a polyisoprenylated benzophenone derivative, is one of the major active constituents isolated from the Garcinia indica fruit. Garcinol inhibits the HAT activity of p300, reducing oxidative stress and inflammation in cisplatin-induced acute kidney injury [ ].

## Berberine
Berberine, a quaternary isoquinoline-based alkaloid, an active constituent isolated from Coptis rhizome. The inhibition of p300 by Berberine alters the global acetylation pattern of LPS-induced macrophage protein acetylation and reduces the acetylation of NF-κB subunit p65 at Lys310 site (p65Lys310), resulting in the inhibition of NF-κB translocation and transcriptional activity, thereby suppressing the expression of inflammatory factors [ ].

## Gaps in p300 research and future directions
P300 and its homologous protein CREB binding protein (CBP) form a highly overlapping and cooperative complex network in vivo, which is the core reason for the difficulty in developing broad-spectrum inhibitors of P300. P300 and CBP are not simply redundant, but play partially overlapping but indispensable roles in maintaining cellular identity and function. p300/CBP acts as massive scaffold proteins that bind to multiple transcription factors (such as NF-κB and STATs) simultaneously through their multiple domains, collectively stabilizing transcription initiation complexes. For example, in the differentiation process of human primary myoblasts, although CBP and p300 jointly activate myogenic program, they regulate different gene sets to control multiple aspects of myoblasts biology [ ], indicating that P300 and CBP have unique and irreplaceable specificity in certain key physiological processes, forming a fine and complex regulatory network. Due to the fundamental synergistic effect of p300/CBP complex in embryonic development, cell differentiation, and immune homeostasis, inhibition of both can disrupt a wide range of related gene expression, leading to severe toxicity, including developmental defects and immune dysfunction [ ]. Simultaneously inhibiting p300 and CBP may disrupt both pro-inflammatory (such as Th17) and anti-inflammatory (such as Treg) pathways, leading to immune system imbalance, increasing the risk of infection. The future research and development focus will be on how to achieve specific targeted interventions for the pathogenic function of p300 in specific diseases, without affecting CBP related functions. The main factors to consider include but are not limited to the following: 1) Targeting non-catalytic functions of p300: In addition to HAT activity, p300 also functions through its protein interaction domains (such as the BRD and CH1 domain). Developing small molecule inhibitors targeting these specific domains may be able to more accurately disrupt specific pathogenic protein complexes (such as the interaction between p300 and RORγt [ ]) while preserving their physiological binding with other partner factors. 2) Developing allosteric inhibitors: Searching for allosteric inhibitors that can bind to sites except the HAT domain, selectively inhibiting the binding of p300 to specific transcription factors by changing its conformation, rather than completely shutting down its enzymatic activity. 3) Using protein degradation technology: By designing bifunctional molecules that can simultaneously bind p300 (rather than CBP) and E3 ubiquitin ligase using degradation agent technologies such as PROTAC, the p300 protein itself can be directly degraded. This method can physically remove targets, potentially achieving higher selectivity than catalytic inhibition and completely disrupting p300’s function as a scaffold, providing a new approach for treating diseases driven by p300.
Super enhancers are a group of p300-labeled enhancers that can distinctly upregulate the transcription of target genes, which aids in the identification of key nodes in transcriptional control during cell fate determination. The super-enhancers landscape experiences substantial alterations during cell differentiation and activation. Owing to the crucial role of these processes in the immune responses, p300 labeled super enhancers may aid in identifying new targets for treating inflammatory diseases [ ]. Sen et al. found through high-throughput screening that p300 is a key protein regulating aging-induced inflammation. P300 induces the formation of new super-enhancers that drive the expression of aging-related genes. Therefore, p300 is a promising candidate for treating aging-induced chronic inflammation [ ].
As a key transcriptional co-activator, p300 plays a central yet distinctive role in the differentiation and function of various immune cells through its HAT activity. Existing literature reports that p300 influences inflammatory responses related to macrophages and T cells. In macrophages, p300 primarily promotes M1 polarization by activating the NF-κB pathway, thereby amplifying its mediated inflammation. When macrophages are activated by signals such as LPS or lipoproteins, p300 is recruited to the promoter or enhancer regions of inflammatory genes. It enhances the DNA-binding capacity and transcriptional activity of the NF-κB p65 subunit through acetylation, strongly amplifying the production of key pro-inflammatory factors like TNF-α and IL-6 [ , , ] Additionally, p300 participates in regulating the polarization of M1-type macrophages and the expression of their specific inflammatory mediators (such as CXCL1), thereby recruiting neutrophils during acute inflammation and exacerbating tissue damage [ ]. In T cells, P300 serves as a critical factor in cellular differentiation, particularly in maintaining the balance of the Th17. Multiple studies have shown that P300 enhances the stability and transcriptional activity of the transcription factor RORγt by directly acetylating it, thereby driving the expression of Th17-characteristic cytokines such as IL-17 and promoting inflammation [ , , ].
P300 may play different or even opposing roles in various tissues and cell types, making the risks of systemic administration difficult to predict. Multiple studies have shown that p300 is a key driver of inflammation in alveolar macrophages and airway smooth muscle cells, and inhibiting its activity can effectively alleviate inflammation [ , ]. However, p300 is crucial for maintaining adult muscle integrity, and its deficiency activates inflammation and induces a dystrophic-like phenotype [ ]. This functional conflict across different tissues hinders the development of p300 inhibitors for systemic inflammatory diseases. Additionally, nearly all current data on p300's role in inflammation derive from in vitro models and mouse models, which presents significant uncertainties in translating these findings to human disease treatment. The progression of various inflammatory disease models developed in mice differs from that in humans, necessitating increased pharmacological efficacy and safety studies in primate models.
Based on the above issues and existing foundations, future research directions for p300 in inflammatory diseases should focus on the following aspects: 1) Developing highly selective inhibitors: optimizing the specificity of inhibitors through structural biology and computer-aided design, with special attention to distinguishing them from CBP; 2) exploring tissue targeted delivery, such as nano-carriers or antibody coupling techniques, to enhance the specific enrichment of drugs in diseased tissues; 3) combination therapy strategy: combining p300 inhibitors with existing anti-inflammatory drugs to enhance efficacy and reduce drug resistance; and 4) epigenetic editing techniques, such as the CRISPR-dCas9 system, precisely regulate p300 activity at specific gene loci.

## Conclusion
P300, a key regulatory factor in inflammatory diseases, regulates inflammatory responses by modulating acetylation of histones and transcription factors. Small-molecule inhibitors and natural compounds that target p300 have shown promising therapeutic effects in various inflammatory disease models. Future studies should focus on improving the selectivity of inhibitors, exploring tissue-specific regulatory mechanisms, and promoting their clinical translation to provide new strategies for treating inflammatory diseases.
