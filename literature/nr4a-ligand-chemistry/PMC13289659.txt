## Abstract

## Objective
Salvianic acid A, known for its potent cardioprotective effects, is a promising therapeutic agent for myocardial ischemia-reperfusion (I/R) injury, a condition where ferroptosis plays a critical role. This study aims to explore the effect of Salvianic acid A on I/R injury through EGFR-Nrf2-GPX4-mediated ferroptosis.

## Methods
The levels of ROS, GSH, GSSG, Fe2+, the state of ferroptosis, the expression and localization of Nrf2, and the expression of EGFR were all detected in hypoxia/reoxygenation (H/R) cells. The Nrf2 inhibitor ML385 and siRNA-EGFR were used to confirm the involvement of EGFR/Nrf2 in protecting cardiomyocytes from H/R-induced ferroptosis. Additionally, the regulatory effect of Salvianic acid A on EGFR/Nrf2/GPX4 were analyzed in the I/R animal model with EGFR inhibitors/agonists.

## Results
Salvianic acid A inhibited ROS, Fe2+, GSH, GSSG, GSH/GSSG, and promoted GPX4 and SLC7A11 expressions in H/R cells. Further experiments demonstrated that Salvianic acid A promoted Nrf2 nuclear translocation and expression to inhibit ferroptosis, which was abolished by the Nrf2 inhibitor ML385. In addition, the molecular docking indicated that Salvianic acid A has a good binding activity with EGFR, and cell experiments also confirmed that Salvianic acid A promoted EGFR expression, which was required for its subsequent activation of Nrf2 and inhibition of ferroptosis, as verified by siRNA-EGFR. In vivo experiments showed that Salvianic acid A alleviated myocardial injury, inhibited ferroptosis, and this improvement was related to the regulation of NRF2-GPX4 by EGFR.

## Conclusion
Salvianic acid A mediated ferroptosis through the EGFR-regulated NRF2-GPX4 pathway, ultimately improving myocardial I/R injury.

Keywords: Salvianic acid A, myocardial ischemia-reperfusion injury, EGFR, Nrf2-GPX4, ferroptosis

## Introduction
Myocardial ischemia-reperfusion (I/R) injury poses a significant clinical challenge, as it leads to further structural and functional deterioration after reperfusion therapy for acute myocardial infarction, ultimately contributing to heart dysfunction and death. The pathophysiology of I/R is multifaceted, involving processes such as calcium overload, oxidative stress, inflammatory response, and various forms of cell death. A deeper investigation into its exact molecular mechanisms is therefore essential for developing effective interventions.
Ferroptosis, a novel iron-dependent form of cell death, has been identified as a key contributor to myocardial I/R injury. In contrast to apoptosis and necrosis, ferroptosis is characterized by iron overload, lethal lipid peroxidation, and the inactivation of glutathione peroxidase 4 (GPX4). The transcription factor Nrf2, serves as the primary regulator of cellular antioxidant defenses, can transcriptionally activate the expression of antioxidant gene GPX4, significantly inhibiting ferroptosis. , The Nrf2-GPX4 axis is recognized as a vital endogenous protective pathway against ferroptosis and the progression of I/R injury. The epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR), a classic tyrosine kinase receptor, not only participates in cell proliferation and differentiation but has also been implicated in oxidative stress responses and cell survival. Recent studies have shown that the activation of EGFR can boost cellular antioxidant capacity by promoting the nuclear translocation and transcriptional activity of Nrf2, , thereby upregulating the expression of GPX4 and suppressing ferroptosis. However, it remains unclear whether EGFR can inhibit ferroptosis by changeable the Nrf2-GPX4 axis to mitigate I/R injury in cardiomyocytes.
Salvianic acid A (Danshensu), a phenolic aromatic acid isolated from the traditional Chinese herb Salvia miltiorrhiza, exhibits potent antioxidant and cardioprotective activities. It has been broadly applied in the cardiovascular diseases either alone or in combination, and can treat myocardial infarction by attenuating myocardial fibrosis, suppressing myocardial cell apoptosis, improving cellular metabolism, diminishing inflammatory responses, alleviating oxidative stress, enhancing hemorheology, and promoting angiogenesis. , Moreover, evidence suggests that Salvianic acid A demonstrates anti-ferroptotic activity. It can alleviate brain damage by inhibiting oxidative damage and ferroptosis, in part through regulation of the Nrf2/GPX4 axis. The preliminary molecular docking results have confirmed that Salvianic acid A has a good binding activity with EGFR (Supplementary Figure 1). Although numerous researches have confirmed that Salvianic acid A mitigates I/R injury, it remains unclear whether its protective mechanisms involve regulation of ferroptosis, specifically, whether it precisely modulates the core Nrf2‑GPX4 pathway via EGFR signaling.
The present research aims to systematically elucidate the efficacy of Salvianic acid A on ferroptosis following myocardial I/R injury, and to explore the interplay between EGFR and the Nrf2/GPX4-mediated ferroptotic cascade. Specifically, we will determine whether Salvianic acid A promoted Nrf2 transcriptional activity by activating EGFR, thereby inhibiting myocardial cell ferroptosis, ultimately achieving the protective effect on myocardial I/R injury.

## Material and Methods

## Cell Culture and Treatment
The rat cardiomyocytes H9C2 (no. iCell-r012) were purchased from iCell (China) and cultured in a medium consisting of 89% DMEM (no. PM150210, Procell, China) + 10% FBS (no. C04001-500, Vivacell, China) + 1% Penicillin - Streptomycin mixture (no. S110JV, BasalMedia, China) at 37°C with 5% CO2, and the experiments design was structured to encompass four distinct components. The first part divided into the control group, H/R model group (H/R), H/R + Salvianic acid A low-dose group (200 μM, Salvianic acid A-low, no. HY-N1913, MCE, USA), H/R + Salvianic acid A high-dose group (300 μM, Salvianic acid A-high), H/R + ferroptosis inhibitor Fer-1 (20 μM, Fer-1, no. HY-100579, MCE, USA). The second part included the control, H/R, H/R + Salvianic acid A-low (200 μM), and H/R + Salvianic acid A-high (300 μM) groups. Based on the dose‑response results from the first two parts, the high dose (300 μM) of Salvianic acid A was selected for all subsequent mechanism experiments. The third part included control, H/R, H/R + Salvianic acid A-high (300 μM), and H/R + Salvianic acid A-high + Nrf2 inhibitor ML385 (5 μM, no. HY-100523, MCE, USA) groups. The fourth part included control, H/R, H/R + Salvianic acid A-high (300 μM), H/R + Salvianic acid A-high + siRNA-NC, and H/R + Salvianic acid A-high + siRNA-EGFR groups.
After the cell confluence reached 70%, the medium was switched to high‑glucose DMEM supplemented with 1% FBS for synchronization treatment for 24 h before constructing the hypoxia/reoxygenation (H/R) model, and different treatments were carried out according to the groups. Each experiment was repeated independently at least three times (n=3 independent biological replicates). To establish the H/R model, cells underwent 12 h of hypoxia in serum- and glucose-free DMEM using a three-gas incubator (37°C, 5% CO2, 1% O2, and 94% N2). Following this, reoxygenation was initiated by replacing the medium with DMEM containing 1% FBS and transferring the cells to a normoxic incubator (37°C, 5% CO2, and 95% O2) for 24 h. The drugs in each group were administered for pre-treatment during the 24-hour synchronization period prior to H/R induction.

## Cell Transfection
The siRNA-NC and siRNA-EGFR were purchased from GenePharma (China) and transfected the cells to intervene in the expression of EGFR. Approximately 2×105 cells/mL of H9C2 cells were collected and inoculated in 6-well plates at 37°C, 5% CO2 in a constant temperature incubator. After preparing the siRNA and transfection reagents into a transfection complex, it was added to the complete medium without the double antibody for culturing the adherent H9C2 cells. After 6 h of constant temperature culture, the complete medium was changed to continue culture for 42 h.

## CCK8 Analysis
After H9C2 cells were subjected to different treatments, CCK8 reagent (no. BS350B, Biosharp, China) diluted in serum-free medium was added, and the cells were further cultured at 37°C and 5% CO2 for 2 h. The absorbance values of each well were measured using an enzyme reader (no. ELx800, BioTek, USA) at a wavelength of 450 nm, and the cell survival rate was calculated.

## Flow Cytometry
The lipid reactive oxygen species (lipid-ROS) in cell and myocardial tissue samples were detected by flow cytometry. The tissue samples were washed with pre-cooled PBS, then ground with a grinder, and the cell suspension was collected and filtered through a 200-mesh cell sieve. It was centrifuged at 300 g for 5 min, washed with PBS, and then lysed in 3 times the volume of red blood cell lysis solution (no. G2015, Servicebio, China). The cell precipitate was collected. In addition, H9C2 cells from different treatment groups were collected. A pre-prepared 5 μM C11-BODIPY 581/591 staining solution (no. HY-D1301, MCE, USA) was applied to the cells at 37°C for 30 min. After staining, flow cytometry (no. Cytoflex, Beckman, China) was used for analysis.

## Immunofluorescence Staining
The Nrf2 and p-EGFR proteins in rat H9C2 cells were observed through immunofluorescence staining. After washing the cell slides with PBS, the membrane-breaking solution (no. G1204, Servicebio, China) was incubated for 10 min, then washed with PBS. The bovine serum blocking solution (no. GC305010, Servicebio, China) treated for 20 min, the antibodies Nrf2 (1:400, no. 16396-1-AP, proteintech, China), p-EGFR (Y1045) (1:200, no. ab316155, abcam, UK), and p-EGFR (Y1068) (1:200, no. AF5797, Beyotime, China) were incubated at 4°C overnight, followed by incubation with the FITC-labeled goat anti-rabbit antibody (no. GB22303, Servicebio, China) at 37°C for 30 min. The DAPI staining solution (no. G1012, Servicebio, China) was incubated in the dark for 10 min to stain the cell nuclei, and then the mounting agent for anti-fluorescence attenuation was used for mounting. Following microscopy imaging, the mean fluorescence intensity was calculated.

## Real-Time Quantitative Polymerase Chain Reaction
Total RNA was extracted from rat H9C2 cell samples using the total RNA extraction kit (no. BL1665A, Biosharp, China). The PrimeScript RT reagent kit (no. RR047A, Takara, China) removed genomic DNA and transcribed it into cDNA. Then, quantitative PCR was performed using the TB Green Premix Ex Taq II (Tli RNaseH Plus) (no. RR820A, Takara, China) on a QuantStudio real-time PCR system (ThermoFisher, USA). Gene-specific primers are listed in . Relative mRNA levels were quantified using the 2−ΔΔCT method.
 

## Animals Experimental Design
A total of 90 male SPF-grade C57BL/6J mice (6–8 weeks) were obtained from Sipeifu (Beijing) Biotechnology Co., Ltd (China). The animal license number is SCXK (Jing) 2024–0001. The experimental animals were raised in a barrier system with a temperature range of 20°C to 26°C and a relative humidity range of 30% to 70%. There was a 12-h day/night light cycle.
After the animals adapted to the environment, they were randomly divided into the sham operation group (SHAM), I/R model group, I/R + Salvianic acid A treatment group (Salvianic acid A), I/R + Salvianic acid A + EGFR inhibitor AG1478 group (Salvianic acid A + AG1478), and the I/R + Salvianic acid A + EGFR agonist EGF group (Salvianic acid A + EGF). Randomisation was performed using a random number table generated by SPSS 22.0. To minimise potential confounders, cage locations were rotated weekly, and the order of treatment administration and sample collection was randomised across groups. The investigator performing the surgeries, administering treatments, and conducting outcome assessments was blinded to group allocation.
Each group of mice was intervened with the corresponding drug before the operation, with Salvianic acid A (50 mg/kg) given via intraperitoneal injection for 14 consecutive days, and AG1478 (30 mg/kg, no. HY-13524, MCE, USA) and EGF (1 mg/kg, no. HY-P7067, MCE, USA) administered by intraperitoneal injection for 7 days. After the drug intervention, except for the Sham group, all the other groups of mice underwent the I/R model construction. The mice were anesthetized with 1% 50 mg/kg pentobarbital sodium, the pain response of the mice was checked, and once it disappeared, the surgery could begin. The small animal ventilator (no. DHX-100, Chengdu Instrument Factory, China) and external light source (no. 76312, RWDLS, China) were turned on, and the heart was exposed to perform coronary artery left anterior descending branch threading to tighten. When the mouse’s heart apex turned white, the thread was loosened after 30 min of ischemia, and blood flow was reperfused for 2 h. The sham group only threaded without tying. No a priori sample size calculation was performed. The sample sizes (sham: n=10; I/R, Salvianic acid A, Salvianic acid A+AG1478, Salvianic acid A+EGF: n=20 each) were determined based on previous published studies and our pilot experiments, which estimated a ~30–40% mortality rate in the I/R model. In the end, the sham group included 10 mice, with 10 surviving. The I/R, Salvianic acid A, Salvianic acid A + AG1478, and Salvianic acid A + EGF groups each included 20 mice, with 11, 14, 11, and 16 surviving, respectively. Animals that died during or immediately after surgery due to surgical failure (eg, excessive bleeding, failed coronary artery ligation) were excluded from the analysis. No other exclusion criteria were applied. After 24 h of I/R, cardiac ultrasound was performed on the mice to measure left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) and left ventricular shortening rate (LVFS). Echocardiography at 24 h post‑reperfusion is a widely accepted standard time point for assessing acute cardiac dysfunction following I/R injury, , which allows direct correlation with molecular analyses performed at the same time point. After the experiment, the mice were deeply anesthetized via intraperitoneal injection of pentobarbital sodium (50 mg/kg), and subsequently euthanized by cervical dislocation after the animal lost consciousness, and the heart samples were collected.

## TTC Staining
Four slices of mouse heart tissue, each with a thickness of 1 mm, were taken and placed in the preheated TTC solution (no. BMB2085, Bomeibio, China) at 37°C for 30 min. The heart slices were then washed with PBS, fixed with 10% neutral formaldehyde for 6 h, and photographed.

## Pathological HE Staining
The fixed tissues of the heart were dehydrated by an automatic dehydration machine (no. JT-12S, WHJJ, China), embedded in paraffin, sectioned, then deparaffinized to water, stained with hematoxylin (no. H9627, Sigma-Aldrich, USA) for 5–10 min, differentiated with hydrochloric alcohol solution for 3 s, re-blotched with weakly alkaline solution, and stained with alcohol-soluble eosin (no. YE2080, BomeiBio, China) for 3 min. The sections were dehydrated with graded alcohol, transparented with a transparent solution, sealed with neutral gum, and observed and photographed under a microscope (no. SQS-600P, Teksqray, China).

## TUNEL Staining
The tissue sections were deparaffinized to water, then subjected to citrate microwave (no. GA2307051, Servicebio, China) repair for 10 min. After incubation with the fluorescent TUNEL solution (no. 1168479590, Roche Group, Switzerland) at 37°C for 1 h, they were washed with PBS, stained with DAPI for 15 min, and then observed by a microscope.

## Prussian Blue Staining
After dewaxing the myocardial tissue sections to water, they were stained with freshly prepared Perls working solution (no. G1424, Solarbio, China) for 30–60 min, washed with distilled water, stained with eosin for 15–30 s, and rinsed with distilled water for 2–3 s. They were then dehydrated through a gradient of alcohol, cleared with a clearing agent, and mounted with neutral gum. The sections were observed and photographed under a microscope.

## Immunohistochemical Staining
The paraffin sections of mouse heart were dewaxed to water, subjected to antigen retrieval by citrate microwave (GA2307051, Servicebio, China), and then treated with 3% hydrogen peroxide (no. G1204, Servicebio, China) to block endogenous peroxidase. The sections were blocked with BSA (no. GC305010, Servicebio, China) for 20 min, reacted with the antibodies EGFR (1:100; no. AF6043, affinity, China), GPX4 (1:200; no. 67763-1-IG, proteintech, China), and Nrf2 (1:100; no ER1706, HUABIO, China) at 4°C overnight, and then incubated with the HRP-labeled goat anti-mouse (1:100; no. GB23301, Servicebio, China) or anti-rabbit (1:100; no. GB22303, Servicebio, China) at 37°C for 30 min. After DAB staining (no. ZL1-9018, ZSbio, China), the nuclei were stained with hematoxylin (no. LM10N13, J&K Scientific, China) for 3 min. Finally, the microscope (no. SQS-600P, Teksqray, China) collected images of the sections and counted the number of positive cells.

## Transmission Electron Microscope
Rat H9C2 cells and mouse myocardial tissues from different treatment groups were pre-fixed with 2.5% glutaraldehyde and then further fixed with 1% osmium tetroxide. After sequential dehydration with acetone, the dehydration agent and Epon-812 embedding agent penetrated, and Epon-812 pure embedding agent was used for embedding. The ultra-thin sections were made using an ultrathin sectioning machine (no. UC7rt, LEICA, Germany) and transferred onto copper slides. The sections were stained with acetic uranyl for 10–15 min and then stained with citrate lead for 1–2 min. The images were collected using a transmission electron microscope (no. JEM-1400FLASH, JEOL, Japan) to observe the presence of ferroptosis characteristics and changes in mitochondrial morphology and structure.

## Elisa

The homogenate is centrifuged at 5000 g for 10 min, and the supernatant is taken for subsequent detection. All operations were carried out in accordance with the instructions of the reagent kit, including the Fe2+ kit (no. BC5415, Solarbio, China), Rat GSH ELISA kit (no. ZC-38269, ZCIBIO, China), Mouse GSH ELISA kit (no. ZC-38193, ZCIBIO, China), and Mouse GSSG ELISA kit (no. ZC-38909, ZCIBIO, China).

## Statistical Analysis
All the experimental data were analyzed using SPSS 22.0 software (IBM Corp., Armonk, NY, USA), and were expressed as the mean ± standard deviation (SD). The assumptions of normality (Shapiro–Wilk test) and homogeneity of variances (Levene’s test) were examined for each dataset. In all cases, both assumptions were met. Therefore, comparisons among multiple groups were performed using one‑way ANOVA followed by Tukey’s post hoc test for multiple comparisons. Two‑group comparisons were analyzed using independent samples t‑test. The results were considered to have significant differences between groups when P < 0.05.

## Results

## Salvianic Acid A Inhibited Ferroptosis Induced by H/R in H9C2 Cardiomyocytes
The optimal dose of Salvianic acid A for H/R-induced H9C2 cells was screened by CCK8 assay, as shown in , the cell activity in the H/R group was meaningfully reduced (P < 0.001). Salvianic acid A significantly promoted cell viability starting from 150 µM (P < 0.05), with a highly significant difference at 200 µM (P < 0.001), and this promoting effect increased with the increase in the dosage, reaching a peak at 300 µM and then decreased (P < 0.001). Therefore, 200 µM was selected as the low-dose group and 300 µM as the high-dose group for the subsequent experiments. The ferroptosis inhibitor Fer-1 was used to compare the effects of Salvianic acid A on cell ferroptosis. It showed that the levels of ROS ( ) and Fe2+ ( ) in the H/R group pointedly upregulated (P < 0.001), while Salvianic acid A-low, Salvianic acid A-high, and the Fer-1 groups significantly inhibited their levels in H/R cells (P < 0.05). Additionally, the content of GSH ( ) and GSH/GSSG ( ) in the H/R group significantly decreased (P < 0.001), while GSSG ( ) significantly increased (P < 0.001). The Salvianic acid A-low, Salvianic acid A-high, and the Fer-1 groups significantly promoted GSH and GSH/GSSG in H/R cells, and inhibited GSSG (P < 0.05, ). TEM results showed that mitochondria in H/R cells were significantly contracted in the cytoplasm, the rough endoplasmic reticulum expanded in a cystic shape, and ferroptosis characteristic changes were observed ( ). The mitochondria in the samples of the Salvianic acid A-low group were contracted, while those in the Salvianic acid A-high and the Fer-1 groups were slightly contracted, and the ferroptosis characteristics were reduced compared to the H/R group ( ). WB results displayed that the expressions of ferroptosis-related marker proteins GPX4 and SLC7A11 were significantly decreased in the H/R group (P < 0.001, and ). The Salvianic acid A-low, Salvianic acid A-high, and the Fer-1 groups significantly promoted their expressions in the H/R cells (P < 0.001, and ).
 To further characterize the protective effect of Salvianic acid A, the cardiomyocyte‑specific injury marker cardiac troponin I (cTnI) was measured in the cell culture supernatant. As shown in Supplementary Figure 2A, H/R injury significantly increased cTnI levels, and Salvianic acid A treatment (both low and high doses) markedly reduced cTnI release, comparable to the ferroptosis inhibitor Fer‑1 (P < 0.01). Additionally, to determine whether the protective effect of Salvianic acid A is specific to ferroptosis, the expressions of cleaved-caspase‑3 (apoptosis) and CHOP (ER stress‑induced apoptosis) were examined by Western blot. As shown in Supplementary Figure 2B, H/R injury significantly increased both cleaved-caspase‑3 and CHOP expression (P < 0.001). However, Salvianic acid A treatment did not reduce their expression (P > 0.05), indicating that the protective effect of Salvianic acid A is not mediated by inhibition of apoptosis or ER stress, but rather specifically through inhibition of ferroptosis. In summary, ferroptosis was activated in H/R, while Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited this process.

## Salvianic Acid A Promoted Nrf2 Expression and Nuclear Translocation in H9C2 Induced by H/R
Nrf2 is regarded as an important transcriptional regulator that plays a role in anti-ferroptosis. The immunofluorescence results showed that the expression of Nrf2 was pointedly decreased in the H/R group (P < 0.001, and ), and it was mainly expressed in the cytoplasm ( ). The Salvianic acid A-low and Salvianic acid A-high groups significantly promoted the expression of Nrf2 and induced their nuclear translocation in the H/R group (P < 0.001, and ). The protein detection results showed that the expression of Nrf2 in the nuclear protein and total protein of the H/R group was significantly decreased, and the Salvianic acid A-low and Salvianic acid A-high groups significantly promoted its expression in the nuclear and total protein of the H/R group (P < 0.001, and ). In addition, the protein expressions of HO-1 and NQO1 in the H/R group were significantly reduced, and the Salvianic acid A-low and Salvianic acid A-high groups significantly promoted their protein expressions in the H/R group (P < 0.001, and ). The gene detection results also showed that the Salvianic acid A-low and Salvianic acid A-high groups significantly promoted the gene expressions of HO-1 and NQO1 in the H/R group (P < 0.01, ), but not affect the gene level of Nrf2 (P > 0.05, ).
 

## Salvianic Acid A Protected H/R-Induced Ferroptosis by Activating Nrf2
To further confirm the role of Nrf2 in the protection of cardiomyocytes from H/R-induced ferroptosis by Salvianic acid A, the Nrf2 inhibitor ML385 was used for treatment. The CCK8 results showed that the cell viability in the H/R group was significantly decreased, while Salvianic acid A significantly promoted the cell viability in the H/R group (P < 0.001, ). But ML385 did not affect the promoting effect of Salvianic acid A on cell viability (P > 0.05, ). As shown previously, the ROS content in the H/R group was significantly increased, while Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited the ROS content in the H/R group (P < 0.001, ). And ML385 reversed the inhibitory effects of Salvianic acid A on the ROS content in the H/R cells (P < 0.001, ). Similarly, ML385 reversed the regulatory effect of Salvianic acid A on the contents of GSH, GSSG, and GSH/GSSG in the H/R cells (P < 0.05, ). Moreover, ML385 meaningfully repressed the enhancement of Nrf2 expression both in the nuclear and total proteins induced by Salvianic acid A in H/R cells (P < 0.001, and ), as well as the promoting effects on GPX4 and SLC7A11 (P < 0.001, and ). This confirmed that Salvianic acid A inhibited H/R-induced ferroptosis by activating Nrf2.
 

## Salvianic Acid A Promoted EGFR Activation Induced by H/R in H9C2 Cardiomyocytes
Activation of EGFR has been proven to have a protective effect on myocardial I/R injury. Molecular docking revealed that the binding affinity of Salvianic acid A to EGFR was −5.03 kcal/mol, indicating that Salvianic acid A has a good binding potential with EGFR. Specifically, Salvianic acid A forms electrostatic forces with EGFR residues ARG752 and ARG681, and hydrogen bonds with ALA743, MET742, LEU680, LEU753, LEU679, LEU754, GLY755, and TRP707, suggesting a good binding activity between them (Supplementary Figure 1). The detection results of EGFR and its phosphorylated protein expression showed that EGFR expression did not differ significantly across any of the cell groups (P > 0.05, and ). However, the expressions of p-EGFR (Y1045) and p-EGFR (Y1068) in the H/R group were significantly decreased, while the Salvianic acid A-low and Salvianic acid A-high groups significantly promoted their expressions in the cells of the H/R group (P < 0.001, and ). The immunofluorescence findings aligned with the WB results. That is, Salvianic acid A significantly promoted the levels of p-EGFR (Y1045) and p-EGFR (Y1068) in the cells of the H/R model group (P < 0.001, ).
 

## Salvianic Acid A Improved H/R-Induced Cardiomyocyte Ferroptosis by Activating EGFR
SiRNA-EGFR was used to silence EGFR expression in cells, to verify the role of EGFR activation in the improvement of cardiomyocyte ferroptosis induced by H/R by Salvianic acid A. Results showed that siRNA-EGFR markedly blocked the enhancement of H/R cell activity by Salvianic acid A and also negated its suppressive impact on ROS and Fe2⁺ levels (P < 0.05, ). Similarly, siRNA-EGFR significantly inhibited the regulatory effects of Salvianic acid A on GSH, GSSG, and the ratio of GSH/GSSG in H/R cells (P < 0.05, ). TEM analysis revealed that siRNA-EGFR markedly attenuated the suppressive effect of Salvianic acid A on ferroptosis in H/R cells ( ). It was confirmed that Salvianic acid A inhibited H/R-induced cardiomyocyte ferroptosis by activating EGFR.
 To directly test whether the protective effect of EGFR is specifically mediated through ferroptosis, a rescue experiment was performed using the ferroptosis inhibitor Fer‑1 in combination with EGFR knockdown (Supplementary Figure 2C–E). It found that EGFR knockdown (siRNA‑EGFR) abolished the protective effect of Salvianic acid A on cell viability, and addition of Fer‑1 fully rescued cell viability to levels comparable to the Salvianic acid A group (P < 0.001, Supplementary Figure 2C). Similarly, Supplementary Figure 2D showed that siRNA‑EGFR increased lipid‑ROS levels, and Fer‑1 completely reversed this effect (P < 0.001). Supplementary Figure 2E demonstrated that siRNA‑EGFR increased Fe2⁺ accumulation, which was fully reversed by Fer‑1 (P < 0.01). These results provide direct evidence that the protective effect of EGFR activation is specifically mediated through inhibition of ferroptosis.
In summary, Salvianic acid A improved H/R-induced cardiomyocyte ferroptosis by activating EGFR, and this protective effect was specifically mediated through inhibition of ferroptosis.

## Salvianic Acid A Promoted the Nuclear Translocation and Expression of Nrf2 by Activating EGFR
Nrf2 is believed to be located downstream of the EGFR signaling pathway. The analysis of Nrf2 localization showed that siRNA-EGFR significantly inhibited the promoting effects of Salvianic acid A on the nuclear translocation of Nrf2 in H/R cells ( ), but had no significant effect on its fluorescence intensity (P > 0.05, ). The WB detection results showed that siRNA-EGFR meaningfully repressed the promoting effects of Salvianic acid A on the expression of Nrf2 in nuclear and total proteins of H/R cells (P < 0.001, ), and also repressed the expression of EGFR in H/R cells treated with Salvianic acid A (P < 0.001, and ). In addition, siRNA-EGFR significantly inhibited the promoting effects of Salvianic acid A on p-EGFR (Y1045), p-EGFR (Y1068), ferroptosis protein GPX4, and SLC7A11 in H/R cells (P < 0.001, and ). In addition, the gene detection results also showed that siRNA-EGFR meaningfully repressed the encouraging effect of Salvianic acid A on the gene levels of Hmox1 and Nqo1 in H/R cells (P < 0.01, ). This confirmed that Salvianic acid A promoted the nuclear translocation and expression of Nrf2 in H/R cells by activating EGFR, ultimately inhibiting ferroptosis.
 

## Salvianic Acid A Improved Myocardial I/R Injury in Mice via EGFR
Further, the effects of Salvianic acid A on myocardial I/R were analyzed through in vivo experiments using EGFR inhibitor AG1478 and agonist EGF. The echocardiographic results of the mice were shown in . The statistical results indicated that the left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) and left ventricular short-axis shortening rate (LVFS) of I/R mice were significantly decreased (P < 0.001), while Salvianic acid A significantly improved both LVEF and LVFS (P < 0.001), and this improvement effect was reversed by AG1478 and further enhanced by EGF (P < 0.05, ). The results of mouse TTC staining were shown in , which found that the myocardial infarct size in I/R mice significantly increased, Salvianic acid A significantly reduced the myocardial infarct size, and this inhibitory effect was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, ). The pathological staining results show that, the model group had myocardial fiber degeneration and necrosis, with mild inflammatory cell infiltration in the necrotic areas or interstitium, fibrous tissue hyperplasia was observed, and fibroblasts with long oval nuclei and fibrocytes with long spindle-shaped nuclei were visible ( ). And the degree of lesions in the Salvianic acid A, AG1478, and EGF group was all reduced to varying degrees, the effect of the EGF was superior to that of the Salvianic acid A group, and the Salvianic acid A group was superior to the AG1478 ( ). Furthermore, the percentage of apoptotic cells in I/R mice significantly increased, Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited cell apoptosis, and this inhibitory effect was reversed by AG1478 (P < 0.01, and ). Similarly, the contents of CK, CK-MB, and cTnI in the serum of I/R mice significantly increased, Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited their contents, and this inhibitory effect was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, ).
 

## Salvianic Acid A Improved Myocardial Ferroptosis in I/R Mice via EGFR
TEM was used to observe the ferroptosis of myocardial cells, and found that the cristae of mitochondria were split, the matrix was lost, the integrity of the inner and outer membranes of mitochondria was lost, presenting vacuolation, and the mitochondria were significantly abnormal, showing the characteristics of ferroptosis in I/R mice ( ). After treatment with Salvianic acid A, the ferroptosis state of the I/R mice was significantly improved ( ). EGF further promoted the improvement effect of Salvianic acid A on ferroptosis, while AG1478 inhibited its effects ( ). The Prussian blue staining displayed that the Fe2+ content in myocardial cells of the I/R group significantly increased, and Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited the iron ion content (P < 0.05, and ). This inhibitory effect was reversed by AG1478, but it was not statistically significant (P > 0.01, and ). Similarly, the ROS content in myocardial tissues of I/R mice meaningfully increased, Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited the ROS content, and this inhibitory effect was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, ). Additionally, the GSSG content in myocardial tissues of the I/R group significantly increased, and GSH and GSH/GSSG significantly decreased (P < 0.001, ). Salvianic acid A significantly regulated their expressions in myocardial tissues of the I/R group, and this regulatory effect was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, ). Immunohistochemistry disclosed that the level of GPX4 in myocardial tissues of the I/R group was significantly decreased, Salvianic acid A significantly increased its expression, and this promoting effect was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, ). Protein detection results were consistent with immunohistochemistry. Salvianic acid A significantly promoted the levels of GPX4 and SLC7A11 in myocardial tissues of I/R mice, and this promoting effect was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, ). In summary, Salvianic acid A inhibited ferroptosis of myocardial cells in mice after myocardial I/R by EGFR.
 

## Salvianic Acid A Activated the EGFR/Nrf2 Pathway in I/R Mice
The immunohistochemical results showed that in the myocardial tissue of the I/R group, the expressions of EGFR and Nrf2 were meaningfully decreased, Salvianic acid A significantly promoted their expressions, and the promoting effect on EGFR was reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, and ). However, AG1478 and EGF had no impact on Nrf2 expression (P > 0.05, and ). WB outcomes showed that the expressions of p-EGFR (Y1045), p-EGFR (Y1068), Nrf2, and p-Nrf2 (S40) were significantly decreased in the I/R mouse myocardium, Salvianic acid A pointedly promoted their expressions, and the promoting effects on p-EGFR (Y1045), p-EGFR (Y1068), and p-Nrf2 (S40) were reversed by AG1478 and further promoted by EGF (P < 0.05, and ). Similarly, AG1478 and EGF had no impact on Nrf2 expression (P > 0.05, and ).
 

## Discussion
Myocardial I/R injury remains a leading contributor to the rising mortality and morbidity associated with cardiovascular diseases. Salvianic acid A, a water-soluble phenolic aromatic acid, exhibits cardioprotective effects. This study demonstrated that Salvianic acid A inhibited ferroptosis induced by H/R in H9C2 cardiomyocytes. Concurrently, it promoted the expression and nuclear translocation of Nrf2 and activated EGFR in H/R-induced H9C2 cells. Further investigations revealed that Salvianic acid A activated EGFR signaling, which promoted Nrf2 nuclear translocation and subsequently up‑regulated the Nrf2/GPX4 pathway, thereby inhibiting ferroptosis in H9C2 cardiomyocytes subjected to H/R and conferring cytoprotective effects. In vivo experiments also confirmed that Salvianic acid A activated Nrf2 through EGFR, promoted the activation of the EGFR/Nrf2/GPX4 to prevent ferroptosis in mice, thereby exerting a protecting effect in I/R injury.
Salvianic acid A, an active constituent extracted from Salvia miltiorrhiza (Danshen), possesses diverse pharmacological properties including coronary artery dilation, anticoagulation, anti-thrombosis, anti-arrhythmia, and anti-myocardial hypertrophy. , Recent studies have verified that Salvianic acid A promotes the polarization of astrocytes and microglia, attenuates neuroinflammation, and ameliorates cerebral ischemic injury, highlighting its potential as a therapeutic agent for cerebral I/R injury. Earlier research further suggested that its cardioprotective effects against I/R injury may be mediated through the inhibition of excessive autophagy and apoptosis via mTOR activation. Additionally, Salvianic acid A has been found to protect the heart from I/R injury by suppressing ROS overproduction through the Sirt1/FoxO1/Rab7 pathway. In line with earlier findings, this study likewise showed that Salvianic acid A markedly enhanced the viability of H/R-treated cardiomyocytes, improved echocardiographic parameters in I/R mice, reduced myocardial infarct size, diminished cardiomyocyte apoptosis, alleviated myocardial injury, and overall exhibited beneficial therapeutic effects against I/R injury. Beyond the heart, studies have also revealed that Salvianic acid A weakened LPS-induced hepatic stellate cell activation during liver fibrosis by modulating ferroptosis, and protected against renal fibrosis by inhibiting ferroptosis. Moreover, Salvianic acid A can bind to Nrf2, augmenting its activity and nuclear accumulation, thereby reducing brain injury by inhibiting oxidative damage and ferroptosis. In line with these observations, our results indicate that Salvianic acid A significantly inhibited ferroptosis in H/R H9C2 cells and I/R mice, while meaningfully promoting the expression and nuclear translocation of Nrf2.
Ferroptosis is a form of cell death driven by iron-dependent oxidative damage, characterized by massive accumulation of Fe2+-dependent lipid peroxides and ROS. Accumulating evidence indicates that ferroptosis contributes to myocardial I/R injury, and ferroptosis inhibitors can significantly reduce cell death, enhance cardiomyocyte viability, and improve cardiac function under I/R conditions. , The ferroptotic process involves dysregulation of iron and lipid metabolism, exacerbated lipid peroxidation, and inactivation of GPX4. Inhibition of ferroptosis has emerged as an effective strategy to mitigate the progression of related diseases. In both the H/R cell model and the I/R animal model employed in this study, cardiomyocytes exhibited a pronounced increase in ROS levels, elevated Fe2+ accumulation, heightened GSH and GSH/GSSG ratios, reduced GSSG content, and markedly decreased expression of GPX4 and SLC7A11. Salvianic acid A significantly suppressed ferroptosis in cardiomyocytes, and this protective effect was associated with activation of Nrf2. As a key CNC-family nuclear transcription factor, Nrf2 plays an essential role in maintaining intracellular redox homeostasis. Studies have identified Nrf2 as a key transcriptional regulator of anti‑ferroptotic defense, enhancing cellular resistance to ferroptosis by up‑regulating genes such as GPX4 and SLC7A11. Our results demonstrate that Salvianic acid A significantly promoted Nrf2 nuclear translocation and expression, leading to up‑regulation of downstream targets HO‑1 and NQO1, and reversing the promoting effect of Salvianic acid A on cell activity and the inhibitory effect on ferroptosis induced by H/R. This confirmed that Salvianic acid A exerts H/R protection by activating Nrf2 to regulate GPX4- and SLC7A11-mediated ferroptosis. Moreover, molecular docking analyses indicated that Salvianic acid A binds to EGFR with high affinity, which corresponded with a significant up‑regulation of phosphorylated EGFR at Tyr‑1045 and Tyr‑1068.
EGFR, a key transmembrane tyrosine kinase receptor of the ErbB2 family, is activated through phosphorylation at specific residues and participates in various processes. Growing evidence suggests that EGFR activation exerts protective effects against myocardial I/R injury. Our findings demonstrate that Salvianic acid A activates EGFR, suggesting that its cardioprotective properties may be mediated, at least in part, through this pathway. Additionally, previous reports have shown that Quiescin sulfhydryl oxidase 1 weakened Nrf2 activation by inhibiting EGFR, supporting the notion that Nrf2 acts downstream of EGFR. Consistently, in both myocardial I/R injury models and oxidative stress-challenged cardiomyocytes, EGF enhances both the protein expression and transcriptional activity of Nrf2. Our data further revealed that EGFR silencing significantly inhibited the promotion effect of Salvianic acid A on Nrf2 nuclear translocation and protein expression in the H/R cell model. Likewise, in I/R mice, pharmacological inhibition of EGFR reversed the Salvianic acid A‑induced up‑regulation of p-Nrf2 (S40), whereas EGFR over‑expression synergized with Salvianic acid A to amplify this effect. In addition, numerous studies indicated that EGFR plays an essential role in regulating cell ferroptosis, and its inhibition promotes ferroptotic cell death in tumor cells, a process linked to altered Nrf2 nucleocytoplasmic distribution and transcriptional activity. Upon phosphorylation at sites such as Tyr‑1045, Tyr‑1068, and Thr‑1069, EGFR facilitates Nrf2 phosphorylation at Ser40, a modification crucial for Nrf2 binding to antioxidant response elements and the subsequent induction of downstream antioxidant genes. Our study showed that EGFR inhibition meaningfully inhibited the levels of p-EGFR (Y1045), p-EGFR (Y1068), and p-Nrf2 (S40), and consequently attenuated the anti‑ferroptotic effect of Salvianic acid A.
In conclusion, Salvianic acid A confers protection against myocardial I/R injury by attenuating ferroptosis through the activation of the EGFR/Nrf2/GPX4 signaling axis.
