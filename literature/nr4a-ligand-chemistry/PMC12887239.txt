## Background
Heart failure is characterized by cardiac dysfunction, cardiac remodeling, and mitochondrial dysfunction. NR4a1 (nuclear receptor subfamily 4 group A member 1) plays a crucial role in regulating mitochondrial function and biological performance in various diseases. The main objective of this study was to investigate the influence of NR4a1 on cardiac hypertrophy and uncover its underlying mechanism.

## Methods
A mouse model was established by transverse aortic constriction surgery, and an in vitro model was established by phenylephrine‐treated neonatal rat ventricular myocytes. Mice were transfected with adeno‐associated virus 9 to assess the role of NR4a1 in cardiac hypertrophy. We used PGC1α (peroxisome proliferator‐activated receptor γ coactivator‐1α) cardiac‐specific knockout mice for subsequent experiments to investigate the impact of NR4a1 on mitochondrial bioenergetics.

## RESULTS
NR4a1 overexpression significantly exacerbates transverse aortic constriction‐induced cardiac remodeling and cardiac dysfunction, and NR4a1 knockdown attenuates cardiac remodeling and cardiac dysfunction. Mechanistically, NR4a1 modulated cardiac remodeling and heart failure by impairing mitochondrial bioenergetics through PGC1α/NRF1 (nuclear respiratory factor 1)/TFAM (transcription factor A mitochondrial) axis. Inhibition of PGC1α activation is critical for NR4a1 to impair mitochondrial bioenergetics in cardiac hypertrophy. Furthermore, cardiac‐specific PGC1α knockdown counteracts heart failure and mitochondrial dysfunction ameliorated by NR4a1 knockdown.

## Conclusions
Collectively, our findings demonstrate that NR4a1 drives pathological cardiac remodeling and heart failure progression by suppressing the PGC1α/NRF1/TFAM axis, leading to impaired mitochondrial function. Targeting NR4a1 and its interactions with PGC1α may hold promise for the development of novel therapeutic strategies for treating cardiac hypertrophy.

## 
NR4a1 Overexpression Aggravates TAC‐Induced Myocardial Hypertrophy, Fibrosis and Cardiac Dysfunction
To further validate the specific effect of NR4a1 on TAC‐induced HF, we established an overexpression mouse model by injecting AAV9‐NR4a1. The overexpression efficiency was confirmed by protein immunoblotting (Figure  ). We executed TAC surgery on NR4a1‐overexpressing mice and assessed viral efficacy in heart after 8 weeks (Figure  ). During the observational period, we recorded the mortality of the mice and found that NR4a1‐overexpressing mice had increased mortality after TAC (Figure  ). Cardiac function analyses revealed that NR4a1 overexpression exacerbated the cardiac dysfunction induced by TAC. Specifically, AAV9‐NR4a1 mice exhibited a significant decrease in LVEF and LV fractional shortening compared with the AAV9‐NC group after TAC. By contrast, the LV internal dimension at end‐diastole and LVIDs, as well as the LV end‐diastolic volume and LV end‐systolic volume, were significantly higher in AAV9‐NR4a1 mice than in AAV9‐NC group mice (Figure  through ). Our results show that the heart weight to body weight ratio, lung weight to body weight ratio, and heart weight to tibial length ratio were significantly upregulated in AAV9‐NR4a1 mice (Figure  ). Next, we analyzed the mRNA levels of cardiac hypertrophy indicators ANP (atrial natriuretic peptide), BNP (B‐type natriuretic peptide), and fibrosis indicators Col I (collagen type I) and Col III, all of which were significantly upregulated in the hearts of AAV9‐NR4a1 mice (Figure  and ). HE staining showed a significant aggravation in the level of myocardial hypertrophy (Figure  ). Consistently, WGA and PSR staining results also showed that NR4a1 overexpression could exacerbate TAC‐induced myocardial hypertrophy and fibrosis (Figure  and ). In summary, these findings suggest that NR4a1 overexpression significantly aggravates TAC‐induced myocardial hypertrophy, fibrosis, and cardiac dysfunction, thereby accelerating the progression to HF.
 

## 
NR4a1 Knockdown Attenuates TAC‐Induced Cardiac Remodeling and Cardiac Dysfunction
Given the pronounced cardiac insufficiency observed in NR4a1 overexpressing mice, we sought to determine the potential protective effect of NR4a1 knockdown in TAC‐induced HF. We used the NR4a1 cardiac‐specific knockdown mouse model (shNR4a1) to perform experiments. The knockdown efficiency was validated after 8 weeks of TAC surgery (Figure  ). As expected, NR4a1 knockdown significantly attenuated TAC‐induced cardiac dysfunction (Figure  and ). Echocardiography results showed that LVEF and fractional shortening were significantly upregulated in NR4a1 knockdown mice after TAC compared with control group, whereas LVIDs, LVIDs, end‐diastolic, and end‐systolic volumes were significantly downregulated (Figure  through ). In addition, HE, WGA and PSR staining showed a significant improvement of myocardial hypertrophy (Figure  , Figure  and ), and the heart weight/body weight, lung weight/body weight and heart weight/tibial length ratios were also significantly decreased in NR4a1 knockdown mice (Figure  ). WGA and PSR staining results consistently demonstrated that NR4a1 knockdown attenuated TAC‐induced cardiac hypertrophy and fibrosis (Figure  and ). We also found that NR4a1 knockdown attenuated cardiac inflammation, as evidenced by reduced inflammatory cell infiltration and lower levels of inflammatory factors (Figure  ). These results suggest that NR4a1 knockdown is potentially protective against TAC‐induced HF.
 

## 
NR4a1 Drives Adverse Cardiac Remodeling and Heart Failure by Impairing Mitochondrial Bioenergetics
Because NR4a1 has been demonstrated to play a regulatory role in mitochondrial fusion‐fission in several disease studies, we hypothesize that the effect of NR4a1 on TAC‐induced HF may also be mediated through the modulation of mitochondrial function. As expected, we examined the mRNA expression levels of genes associated with mitochondrial fusion and division (DRP1, OPA1, Mfn1, Mfn2 and TOM20) and mitochondrial respiratory chain (NDUFB8, SDHB, UQCRC2, MTCO1, and ATP5A1). Our findings revealed that overexpression of NR4a1 exacerbated TAC‐induced myocardial mitochondrial damage and dysfunction, whereas knockdown of NR4a1 ameliorated these deleterious effects (Figure  and ). Subsequently, we employed transmission electron microscopy and observed that overexpression of NR4a1 exacerbated TAC‐induced mitochondrial matrix swelling, along with the shortening and reduction of cristae (Figure  and ). Meanwhile, mitochondrial abundance was also reduced (Figure  ). Consistently, immunoblotting of mitochondrial respiratory chain complex proteins showed that NR4a1 overexpression exacerbated TAC‐induced mitochondrial dysfunction (Figure  ). In vitro experiments consistently demonstrated that the knockdown of NR4a1 in NRVMs attenuated phenylephrine‐induced mitochondrial structural abnormalities (Figure  ). Collectively, these data demonstrate that NR4a1 contributes to cardiac dysfunction primarily by impairing mitochondrial bioenergetics.
 

## 
NR4a1 Inhibits Mitochondrial Bioenergetics by Suppressing PGC1α In Vivo
We next endeavored to elucidate the mechanisms through which NR4a1 contributes to impaired mitochondrial bioenergetics and HF. It is widely recognized that PGC1α plays an important role in mitochondrial biogenesis. , Therefore, we hypothesized that NR4a1 precipitates mitochondrial dysfunction through targeting PGC1α. As expected, tissue immunofluorescence staining showed a reduction in PGC1α expression in mice hearts subjected to TAC in contrast to the sham group, whereas NR4a1 overexpression exacerbated the reduction of PGC1α expression (Figure  ). In addition, the mRNA levels of PGC1α were significantly reduced in TAC‐induced HF, and this reduction was counteracted by the knockdown of NR4a1 (Figure  ). Consistently, protein immunoblotting experiments showed that NR4a1 knockdown rescued the inhibition of PGC1α induced by TAC (Figure  ). Given that PGC1α has been reported to regulate mitochondrial function through the NRF1/TFAM axis, , , our protein immunoblotting and polymerase chain reaction results demonstrated that NR4a1 knockdown counteracts the TAC‐induced reduction in PGC1α and NRF1/TFAM expression, as well as the structural dysfunction of mitochondria (Figure  through ). In summary, these findings suggest that NR4a1 influences mitochondrial function by regulating PGC1α. Specifically, NR4a1 inhibits the expression of PGC1α, which consequently affects mitochondrial structure and function by regulating the NRF1/TFAM pathway.
 

## 
NR4a1 Inhibits Mitochondrial Bioenergetics by Suppressing PGC1α In Vitro
To further elucidate the regulation of PGC1α by NR4a1, we established an NRa1 knockdown model using NRVMs in vitro and validated the knockdown efficiency by protein immunoblotting (Figure  ). Consistent with in vivo experiments, protein immunoblotting results showed that NR4a1 knockdown could counteract phenylephrine stimulation‐induced reduction in the expression of PGC1α and its downstream NRF1/TFAM (Figure  and ). Cellular immunoluminescence also substantiated the relationship between PGC1α and NR4a1. The transcriptional activation of PGC1α, which was suppressed by NR4a1 in response to phenylephrine stimulation, exhibited a reversal of this inhibition in the NR4a1 knockdown group (Figure  ). Furthermore, oxygen consumption rate also showed that NR4a1 knockdown ameliorated phenylephrine‐induced mitochondrial dysfunction in NRVMs, as evidenced by significant improvements in basal, ATP production‐coupled, and maximal respiration (Figure  and ). In conclusion, our in vitro experiments further demonstrated that NR4a1 regulates mitochondrial function by inhibiting the expression of PGC1α.
 

## Activation of PGC1α Transcription Counteracts Impaired Mitochondrial Function Caused by NR4a1 Overexpression In Vitro
To further confirm that NR4a1 affects mitochondrial function by regulating PGC1α, we established an NR4a1 overexpression model in NRVMs and used the PGC1α transcriptional activator ZLN005 for the experiments. As expected, the activation of PGC1α transcription counteracted the diminished expression of NRF1/TFAM and the consequent mitochondrial dysfunction induced by NR4a1 overexpression (Figure  and ). In alignment with this observation, quantitative polymerase chain reaction and immunoblotting results of mitochondrial respiratory chain complex proteins showed that the transcriptional activation of PGC1α rescued mitochondrial dysfunction caused by NR4a1 overexpression (Figure  , Figure  and ). Subsequently, oxygen consumption rate assays further demonstrated that the transcriptional activation of PGC1α rescued the mitochondrial oxidative respiratory dysfunction induced by NR4a1 overexpression (Figure  and ). Taken together, these findings confirm that NR4a1 exerts a detrimental effect on mitochondrial bioenergetics and HF through a PGC1α‐dependent pathway, and the transcriptional activation of PGC1α possesses the potential to ameliorate both mitochondrial and cardiac dysfunctions.
 

## Cardiac‐Specific PGC1α Knockdown Counteracts HF and Mitochondrial Dysfunction Ameliorated by NR4a1 Knockdown
Upon discovering that NR4a1 could regulate HF and mitochondrial bioenergetics by targeting PGC1α, we proceeded to investigate whether cardiac‐specific knockdown of PGC1α could negate the ameliorative effects on HF and mitochondrial function induced by NR4a1 knockdown. Genetic ablation of PGC1α did not significantly induce upregulation of PGC‐1β or PRC (PGC‐1‐related coactivator) expression (Figure  ). We performed NR4a1 knockdown in PGC1α cKO mice at age 8 weeks, then we performed TAC surgery and observed for 8 weeks (Figure  ). We found that PGC1α knockdown in mice effectively counteracted the improvement in cardiac systolic‐diastolic function caused by NR4a1 knockdown, as evidenced by a significant reduction in both LVEF and fractional shortening (Figure  and ). Meanwhile, we found that LVIDs, LV internal dimension at end‐diastole, end‐diastolic volume, and end‐systolic volume were significantly upregulated after NR4a1 knockdown in PGC1α cKO mice, as compared with NR4a1 knockdown in wild‐type mice (Figure  and ). Consistently, HE staining of mouse heart tissues showed a significant increase in myocardial hypertrophy after knockdown of NR4a1 in PGC1α cKO mice compared with NR4a1 knockdown in wild‐type mice (Figure  ). Subsequently, we performed WGA and PSR staining, which found that myocardial hypertrophy and fibrosis levels were significantly improved following NR4a1 knockdown in wild‐type mice. However, this beneficial effect was negated by NR4a1 knockdown in PGC1α cKO mice (Figure  through ). In addition, protein immunoblotting results showed that NR4a1 knockdown ameliorated TAC‐induced mitochondrial dysfunction in comparison to the wild‐type group, but this mitigating effect was significantly diminished in PGC1α cKO mice (Figure  and ). Overall, our experimental results substantiate that NR4a1 modulates HF and mitochondrial function through PGC1α‐dependent pathways. Crucially, the improvement in myocardial remodeling and mitochondrial function conferred by NR4a1 knockdown was negated in PGC1α cKO mice.
 

## DISCUSSION
HF represents a widespread and formidable global health challenge, devoid of effective curative interventions beyond symptomatic relief. Hence, it is important to elucidate the underlying mechanisms of HF progression to improve cardiac function and prognosis. Here we report that the regulator of mitochondrial bioenergetics and cardiac function, NR4a1, is upregulated in mouse hearts experiencing HF induced by TAC. NR4a1 instigates mitochondrial dysfunction in the mouse heart by inhibiting PGC1α expression and then modulating the classical PGC1α pathway, NRF1/TFAM. Cardiac‐specific overexpression of NR4a1 aggravates cardiac dysfunction, myocardial fibrosis, cardiac hypertrophy, and mitochondrial dysfunction. Furthermore, our findings indicate that the ablation of NR4a1 or the transcriptional activation of PGC1α can effectively ameliorate pathological myocardial remodeling by restoring PGC1α expression and mitochondrial bioenergetics. In conclusion, the current investigation enhances our knowledge of the energy‐dependent progression of HF and provide a new therapeutic option for HF treatment by mitigating mitochondrial dysfunction.
As a member of the NR4A nuclear receptor family, NR4a1 is involved in the regulation of inflammatory factor production, fatty acid synthesis and oxidative stress. , , , NR4a1 also undergoes a nuclear‐to‐mitochondrial translocation, a process that triggers mitochondria‐associated cells apoptosis. In myocardial ischemia‐reperfusion injury, NR4a1 has been shown to exacerbate mitochondrial dysfunction. Despite its recognized involvement in energy metabolism across various diseases, the underlying mechanisms by which NR4a1 influences the progression of HF have yet to be investigated. Our study found that NR4a1 was significantly upregulated in TAC‐induced HF and phenylephrine‐treated NRVMs (Figure  ). By constructing cardiac‐specific NR4a1 overexpression and knockdown mice, we demonstrated that NR4a1 directly contributes to HF and cardiac remodeling (Figures  and ). Mitochondrial function plays a pivotal role in the progression of HF. The primary alterations in mitochondrial function observed during HF include decreased mitochondrial biogenesis, decreased mitochondrial viability, increased mitochondrial fission, and decreased mitochondrial fusion. We observed that overexpression of NR4a1 exacerbated mitochondrial structural damage and impaired oxidative respiration in TAC‐induced HF, whereas NR4a1 knockdown produced the opposite effect (Figure  ). Our study highlights NR4a1 as a promising therapeutic target for HF.
Considering the critical role of NR4a1 in the initiation and exacerbation of cardiac dysfunction, we investigated the downstream signaling pathways of NR4a1. As a member of the PGC1 family, PGC1α plays an important role in a variety of biological functions, including inflammation, endoplasmic reticulum stress, oxidative stress, and mitochondrial function. Research has demonstrated that PGC1α regulates mitochondrial function in white adipose tissue and skeletal muscle. , , Moreover, the role of PGC1α in diabetic cardiomyopathy, cardiac hypertrophy, viral myocarditis, and HF has been extensively explored. PGC1α promotes mitochondrial biogenesis through its mediated classical pathway NRF1/TFAM. The expression of PGC1α is tightly regulated by posttranslational modifications, including phosphorylation. AMPK enhances PGC1α activity through phosphorylation, whereas its activity is typically suppressed by Akt, GSK‐3β, and S6K1 via phosphorylation. , Given these findings, we focused specifically on the role of PGC1α in NR4a1‐driven cardiac dysfunction. Interestingly, overexpression of NR4a1 significantly suppressed PGC1α expression both in vivo and in vitro. In contrast, knockdown of NR4a1 restored the expression of PGC1α, which subsequently ameliorated mitochondrial structure and function. The expression of PGC1α was closely correlated with NRF1, TFAM, mitochondrial fusion, and fission proteins (Figures  and ). Research has demonstrated that NR4a1 acts as a deleterious factor, aggravating cardiac injury by promoting mitochondrial fragmentation. , OPA1 is a key regulator of mitochondrial inner membrane fusion, with alterations in its isoform changes (decrease of L‐OPA1 and increase of S‐OPA1) leading to mitochondrial fragmentation and ultimately contributing to HF. , We observed that NR4a1 knockdown prevented the decrease in L‐OPA1 induced by TAC but had no significant effect on the increase of S‐OPA1. Additionally, the detrimental impact of NR4a1 on mitochondrial function was successfully counteracted by the administration of ZLN005, a specific agonist of PGC1α (Figure  ). Additionally, PGC1α cKO abolished the restorative effects of NR4a1 knockdown on cardiac function and mitochondrial function (Figure  ), which suggests that NR4a1 regulates mitochondrial biogenesis by inhibiting PGC1α expression, thereby worsening TAC‐induced HF. In addition to mitochondria, numerous studies have highlighted the role of NR4a1 in inflammatory cells, whereas our research focuses on cardiomyocytes. , Notably, we still observed that knockdown of NR4a1 attenuates TAC‐induced cardiac inflammation. This protective effect may be attributed to the restoration of PGC1α expression and a consequent improvement in mitochondrial function.
This study has several limitations. First, this work has not been validated using human samples and therefore cannot fully replicate the clinical disease. Second, the precise structural domain of NR4a1 that mediates its effect on PGC1α remains to be elucidated in subsequent investigations.

## Conclusions
In conclusion, this study unveils that NR4a1 plays a pivotal detrimental role in myocardial remodeling and HF by inhibiting the PGC1α/NRF1/TFAM signaling axis, leading to suppression of mitochondrial bioenergetics. Considering the profound clinical importance of HF and mitochondrial bioenergetics, our findings not only reinforce the biological function of NR4a1 in cardiovascular diseases but also illuminate novel perspectives on potential therapeutic strategies for HF.
