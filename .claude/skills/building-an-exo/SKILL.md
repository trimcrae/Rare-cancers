## Triggers

- Founder or CEO is asking "what would we build today, with AI, from scratch?"
- The firm has launched isolated AI pilots that produced 20–40% gains and stalled. (Phase 2 trap.)
- AI spend is high but firm-level ROI is flat: token leaderboards, agent counts as KPIs, or "we burned the budget and can't trace it to shipped features." (Tokenmaxxing pattern; only 27% of executives say AI met ROI expectations.)
- Leadership has cut headcount without redesigning the underlying workflows. (Anti-case study from Chapter 11; the Alloy delayering scenario.)
- A board or investor has asked the CEO to publish an AI strategy and the CEO doesn't have a destination architecture.
- A board needs a formal agentic governance framework: decision rights, escalation thresholds, fiduciary liability, disclosure. (The Sarbanes-Oxley Moment.)
- A vendor is pitching a suite as the transformation: "activate the agents in your ERP and you're autonomous." (The Vendor Shortcut.)
- The firm is preparing for or executing a workforce transition affecting more than 10% of staff.
- High-judgment talent is leaving, or retention strategy is salary-matching in a remote-first firm. (The Binding Problem.)
- An AI agent already operates somewhere in the business without a documented Permission Envelope or Fiduciary Wedge.
- The firm is single-vendor on foundation models, orchestration, or the whole stack including systems of record. (Cognitive captivity.)
- A high-coordination, low-judgment workflow has been identified as a candidate for an MVIS pilot.
- The firm is over 50 employees and any AI initiative is stalling inside an existing business unit. (Immune system signal.)
- The firm is under 50 employees and the founder is debating whether to grow headcount or grow agency.
- Intelligence density (revenue per employee, throughput per person, ARR growth per dollar of net burn) is on the diagnostic table.
- A near-miss has occurred: production data damaged by an agent, an autonomous decision outside the envelope, an unsafe handover. (PocketOS pattern.)
- The CEO needs to decide between Direct Mode and Edge Mode and is reaching for the wrong one.
- The board is panicking at the trough of the dual-cost J-curve and wants to kill the edge initiative.
- Mission-driven entity (government, non-profit) needs an Edge Mode adaptation that respects the immune-system-is-law constraint.

## Core Directives

### The Three Things to Remember

- MUST anchor every conversation in the three-part frame: **Destination (ExO 3.0)**, **Operating System (Intelligence Stack)**, **Playbook (REWRITE)**. Skip the rest of the framework if needed; never skip these three.
- MUST hold the v24 automotive analogy when explaining how the three relate: **the Intelligence Stack is the engine block** (the operating core everything plugs into), **DRIVE is the drivetrain** (converts cognitive power into speed, options, and traction), **SHAPE is the chassis and safety systems** (the structural resilience that keeps the drivetrain from tearing the firm apart). Build the Stack first.
- MUST refuse the temptation to teach DRIVE without SHAPE or SHAPE without DRIVE. The v24 stakes line: *DRIVE without SHAPE is a fuse waiting for a spark.*

### The Appendix A Diagnostics Suite (run before architecture work)

- MUST run the consolidated v24 diagnostics suite, in order, before Chapter 3 work begins: Readiness Score (with anchored 1/10 descriptors) → Score Interpretation Matrix → Miura-Ko reconciliation → Dabbling Test → Third Anchor → Tokenmaxxing Test. **Structural note: the CEO Quick Start no longer hosts these tests; Appendix A does.** Use `templates/rewrite-readiness-scorecard.md`.
- MUST apply the **Dabbling Test** as a strict binary with both checks: the 50% Time Check (Krivkovich: *"If 50% of my time isn't spent differently because I can access AI to do my job, I'm dabbling."*) and the Operating-Cadence Check (approval chains, offsites, operating reviews, capital allocation materially changed). MUST cite the v24 metric convergence: per Jenkins (OpenText), "stop measuring how many employees use AI assistants, and start measuring the volume of workflows safely executed by autonomous agents under human command."
- MUST apply the **Tokenmaxxing Test** (operational companion to the Dabbling Test) whenever spend is high and ROI is flat: **Leaderboard** (input-side proxies rewarded = paying for Goodhart's Law), **Geometry** (agents preserving the old org chart = group drive on a steam-era shop floor), **Latency** (tasks 5x faster but cycle time unchanged = congestion). A single Yes places the firm below L3 regardless of spend. *"Three Yeses, or three Don't-Knows, equal transformation theater regardless of spend."* The fix is Chapter 6 plus REWRITE Step 4, never a new tool purchase.
- MUST check the **Third Anchor: workforce capacity** (Mercer: thriving at 44%, down from 66% in 2024). Neither the Dabbling Test nor the ladder reads accurately on a depleted human substrate.
- MUST locate the firm on the **Miura-Ko L0–L5 ladder** alongside the Readiness Score: L0 Theater, L1 Personal Productivity, L2 Team Workflow, L3 Organizational Infrastructure (where the architecture starts to compound), L4 Compounding Operating System (where Value Moats form), L5 Virtually Self-Driving (does not yet exist). **Trust-the-ladder rule:** if score and level diverge, trust the ladder; capacity un-operationalized doesn't compound. A high score with a low level is the signature of a firm that has purchased the architecture but not deployed it.

### ExO 3.0: The Destination Architecture

- MUST replace ExO 1.0's SCALE/IDEAS split with the ten-characteristic ExO 3.0 frame: MTP plus DRIVE (5) plus SHAPE (5). The internal/external distinction is obsolete; the firm boundary has collapsed.
- MUST encode MTP as a **three-layer machine-readable protocol**, not a poster:
  - **Constraint Layer**, what agents are categorically forbidden from doing. Hard constraints, not aspirational values.
  - **Decision Layer**, weighted priorities agents use when facing tradeoffs.
  - **Identity Layer**, the cultural cohesion mechanism that replaces "the office," carrying **explicit disqualifiers** (v24): the values and motivations that make someone a poor fit, alongside the affirmative pull.
- MUST apply all **three Purpose Litmus Tests (v24)** before signing off:
  1. *Could an AI agent, given only your MTP protocol, make a decision your leadership team would endorse?* If no, your MTP is a poster.
  2. *Could that agent, given only your MTP, decide what NOT to build?* When execution is nearly free, the feature factory is the dominant failure mode.
  3. *Could a high-judgment human, reading only your Identity Layer, answer why they stay, what their contribution makes visible, and who the organization is not for?*
- MUST name the **Agentic Fidelity Paradox** (Delphi Group, 2026) as the failure mode the MTP layers prevent: "the more precisely agents adhere to predefined procedure, the less capable they become on novel problems." Encode purpose, not procedure; let GOVERN catch the drift.
- MUST enforce the Five Design Conditions as principled anchors, not KPIs: AI-Centric Workflow Architecture, Recursive Improvement Infrastructure, Model Sovereignty and Governed Autonomy, Intelligence Density at Every Layer, Human Flourishing as a Binding Constraint. If any condition is violated, the architecture fails. They are a binding Step-1 exit gate.
- MUST locate every advantage on the **Three Compounding Loops** (Intelligence: D → I → R → V; Trust: E-Trust → E-Agency → V; Governance: S → A → R) and verify that no loop is being optimized at another's expense. The Intelligence Loop creates advantage. The Trust Loop scales it. The Governance Loop keeps it from collapsing under its own velocity. One weak characteristic chokes a loop, and the loop is what produces the compounding return. **Optimizing one loop at another's expense is the dominant ExO 3.0 failure mode.** The v24 CEO directive: diagnose the single weakest characteristic currently bottlenecking your data loops and rebuild it.

### DRIVE: The Intelligence Engine (score 1–5 each, total 25) *(What makes you fast and smart)*

- MUST score and stage all five components in any assessment:
  - **D, Decision Architecture.** Two-way doors get speed; one-way doors get human gating; nothing fragile floats in the middle. Map every decision type to who decides, under what conditions, with what guardrails. Cross-check against the **Ju evidence**: 74% of enterprise workflows are monotonic (provably coordination-free); the **Coordination Tax** (the share of coordination spending that buys no correctness at all) runs 24–57%; **congestion** (Azhar: "the buildup of accelerated individual work waiting on an unchanged decision layer") is the operational fingerprint.
  - **R, Recursive Learning.** Workflows versioned, performance quantified, improvements codified and propagated. The LEARN layer runs this at machine speed.
  - **I, Intelligence Stack.** The engine block (six layers + GOVERN/ASSURE). In DRIVE scoring, score whether the Stack exists and is operational.
  - **V, Value Moat.** Five sources: proprietary data, network effects, intelligence density, reconfiguration speed, curatorial judgment. Any moat that depends on customer inertia is a wasting asset; price it and plan its replacement. Cite the v24 Cognition Labs receipt as "massive ARR with minimal headcount" (the 73× figure was removed in v24; do not use it).
  - **E, Elastic Agency.** Capability Registry, Graduated Authority, **Decision Boundary Map** (v24 rename). Apply sliding talent ratios by sector (Information-centric / Hybrid Operations / Regulated Environments); expect roughly 10 points of shift toward AI every 10 months.
- MUST apply the **GOVERN-cap rule**: a high DRIVE score in the absence of GOVERN/ASSURE is overstated. Cap the DRIVE total at 13/25 until GOVERN exists in alert-only mode at minimum. This is the PocketOS lesson institutionalized.
- MUST score the **Four Pillars of GOVERN/ASSURE** as a sub-rubric inside I: Trusted Evals, Searchable Logs with Correlation IDs, Granular Rollback, Human Review Queue. **Cap the I score at the lowest pillar.** Most companies score 1s on at least three pillars. **Do not deploy a new agent class until each pillar scores ≥ 3.**
- MUST flag **Quiet Drift** (canonical v24 term; the dominant production failure mode): agents do not crash, they degrade slowly. Detection requires the Trusted Evals pillar with quantified thresholds (accuracy floor + override-rate ceiling). Absent eval suites, drift is discovered in customer escalations rather than dashboards.
- MUST flag **cognitive captivity**: if the Stack runs on a single provider's foundation models and infrastructure, the moat is around someone else's castle. Maintain inference capability across at least two model families. Own orchestration logic and fine-tuning data. The v24 escalation: when one vendor supplies systems of record, orchestrators, AND the governance plane, the autonomy ceiling is bound to their roadmap.
- MUST deliver the **Vendor Shortcut** answer whenever a suite vendor (SAP's Autonomous Enterprise is the named case) is pitched as the transformation. Three structural reasons the shortcut fails: **Group Drive vs. Unit Drive** ("A rented agent catalog cannot redesign your floor boundaries"), **Utility Bill vs. Value Moat** (identical vendor catalogs are a software utility bill), **Cognitive Captivity**. The directive: "Buy the vendor suite if it makes your legacy mothership cheaper to operate. Do not confuse it with the structural rewrite of your operating model." Close with the falsifiable 2028 bet: *"We are betting on the edge; legacy suites are betting you will never change the org chart. One of us is wrong."*
- MUST flag **customer-side agent inversion**: design for the agent buyer, not just the human buyer. Pricing, APIs, contract terms, SLAs, increasingly read by agents on behalf of customers. The slow side of an agent-to-agent negotiation loses by definition.
- MUST surface the **Block warning** when peer firms cite Dorsey's three-role structure as a template: Block is the canonical **DRIVE-without-SHAPE** case at corporate scale (4,000 roles, ~40% of corporate mass, one quarter; canonical home Chapter 9). Ask three questions in order. *Where is your Fiduciary Wedge? Where are the Four Pillars? What is your kill-switch architecture?* Silence is the warning.

### SHAPE: The Organizational Form (score 1–5 each, total 25) *(What keeps you right and resilient)*

### The Intelligence Stack: The Operating Core

- MUST treat the Stack as **Boyd's OODA loop operationalized as enterprise architecture and run continuously at machine speed**. Six cognitive layers plus a cross-cutting control plane:

  - **INTERPRET**, builds context, retrieves history, simulates scenarios (Orient).

### REWRITE: The Migration Playbook (six steps, sequence non-negotiable)

  1. **BACKCAST & DEFINE**, produce a Destination Architecture document from a 2–3 day facilitated executive workshop using the Appendix B canvas, Sections A–E (Current System Anchors / The 2031 Horizon State / Variance Mapping / No-Regret Architecture Moves / Signposts and Trigger Conditions).   2. **ASSESS & PREPARE**, run the Readiness Score across eight dimensions with the v24 anchored descriptors and the Score Interpretation Matrix (56–80 ready; 33–55 foundational; below 33 survival risk, stand up an MVIS backbone). **Add the Four Pillars Maturity sub-rubric (minimum ≥ 3 to deploy new agents) and the Miura-Ko reconciliation (if score and ladder diverge, trust the ladder).** Run the full Appendix A suite including the Dabbling, Third Anchor, and Tokenmaxxing tests. Retake every six months. Choose on-ramp (MVIS / 90-Day Sprint / Full REWRITE).
  3. **EXTRACT**, Knowledge Archaeology + Extraction Sprint + Elicitation-First Principle + **Workflow Data Manifest**. The first agent deployed for any human should be an elicitation agent, not a task executor. Handle extraction with transparency and offer transition support as part of the process, not after. **Produce a one-page Workflow Data Manifest for each workflow you intend to migrate**: every data source, why, read or write, sensitivity tier, retention in the twin's memory, named data owner. **The rule is binary: if you cannot state why a workflow needs a field, the Edge Twin does not get it.** The Manifest is a Step 3 exit criterion.
  4. **DIAGNOSE & STRIP**, Zero-Based Organization Audit and **Task Decomposition Matrix** (the single most important diagnostic). Score every task 1–5 for Agent Readiness. Appoint a CAIO with both technical fluency and P&L literacy, reporting directly to the CEO. This is where the Tokenmaxxing fix lands: collapse the decision layer, not just the execution layer.
  5. **BUILD & PROVE**, Decision Handover Waves (low-risk → medium → higher-judgment), executed entirely within the protected boundary of the Edge Twin. Parallel-run-then-deprecate with success criteria defined before the run starts. Never more than 2–3 parallel workflows simultaneously. Budget 10–15% of savings for the People Side of Parallel Runs (transition leader, retraining, severance, dual-staffing); the Bridge Curriculum is funded from inside this envelope. **Cold-start learning protocol:** the parallel run *is* shadow mode.   6. **REWIRE & EVOLVE**, replace the org chart (a latency map) with pod-based intelligence networks on a thin residual accountability hierarchy. - MUST recognize that the timeline is not the point. The sequencing is. A 30-person SaaS company may move through all six steps in under a year; a 10,000-person manufacturer with legacy ERP and union contracts may take two to three years.

## Cross-Skill Workflows

### First-Pass Building-an-ExO Intake
When a founder or CEO asks "what would we build today, with AI, from scratch?":
1. Start here, confirm the three-things frame (Destination, Operating System, Playbook) and the automotive analogy.
2. Run the Appendix A diagnostics suite with `templates/rewrite-readiness-scorecard.md` (Score → Matrix → Ladder → Dabbling → Third Anchor → Tokenmaxxing). Choose Direct vs. Light Edge vs. Full Edge by headcount.
3. Score the firm with `templates/drive-scorecard.md` and `templates/shape-scorecard.md`.
4. Return here, produce a one-page Destination Architecture sketch and a named candidate workflow for Wave 1.

### Stand-Up-MVIS Workflow
When the firm needs the Minimal Viable Intelligence Stack in week one:
1. Start here, confirm the four MVIS components (event bus, agent registry, central logging, one agent per class) plus the **Four Pillars at MVP level** (eval suite scaffold, correlation-ID logging on by default, one rollback path defined, human-review queue with named owner).
2. Author the first Agent Specification using `templates/agent-specification.md` **and the matching Six Questions for each data object the agent will touch using `templates/hido-six-questions.md`.**
3. Return here, confirm GOVERN/ASSURE is alert-only and the kill switch is testable; confirm Quiet Drift detection thresholds are defined for the first production agent class.

### Edge Twin Spawn Workflow (Tier 4–5)
When a >50-person firm needs to launch the Edge Twin:
1. Start here, confirm board mandate and CEO sponsorship; veto if either is missing. Fund from CEO budget or board allocation, never a division budget.
2. If a vendor suite is being positioned as the alternative, deliver the **Vendor Shortcut** from `references/edge-deployment.md` before proceeding.
3. Run the **CIO Edge Twin Diagnostic** from `templates/cio-edge-twin-diagnostic.md` *before* funding, under the **Readiness Gate Protocol**: any Red on questions 5, 6, 7, or 8 legally halts the build.
4. Produce the **Workflow Data Manifest** for each candidate Wave 1 workflow using `templates/workflow-data-manifest-template.md`. Confirm the binary rule: no field travels to the twin without a stated workflow reason.
5. Confirm the **no-data-fork architecture** from `references/edge-twin-data-governance.md` is signed: workflow-scoped API access, read/write separated, correlation-ID logging, short-lived revocable credentials, ERP wins.
6. Define the **cold-start learning protocol** for the first parallel run using `references/cold-start-learning-feeds.md`. Name the human-override-rate baseline and the falling-rate target.
7. Frame the program with the **portfolio math**: most twins fail; premature termination is the enemy; capture knowledge from every kill.
8. Return here, name the first three workflows to migrate, easiest first, and wire the Self-Disruption Probe to feed the pipeline.

### Workforce-Transition Workflow
When a workforce reduction or restructuring is on the table:
1. Start here, refuse to dress headcount cuts as architecture. If the Destination Architecture isn't signed and the Task Decomposition Matrix isn't scored, stop and route back to Steps 1–4.
2. Take the **delayering steelman** at full strength (Alloy scenario) and answer with the order of operations: the Stack absorbs the coordination first, then the people redeploy.
3. Confirm the People Side of Parallel Runs has 10–15% of savings allocated, a named transition leader, and the **Bridge Curriculum** running from inside that envelope (porosity 30%+ target; caste indicators at board level monthly).
4. Return here, check the **Binding Problem** for the high-judgment cohort you intend to keep: Consequence, Legibility, Identity engineered; pay to parity.

### PocketOS / Quiet Drift Postmortem Workflow
When a failure has occurred, loud (autonomous agent crossed an envelope, destructive action without approval, data loss) **or quiet (Quiet Drift surfaced in customer escalations)**:
1. Start here, name the failure pattern: DRIVE-without-SHAPE, missing GOVERN, blanket-privilege token, missing approval threshold, missing soft-delete window, missing kill switch, **missing Trusted Evals (Quiet Drift)**, **missing Granular Rollback**, or **missing Six-Questions metadata on the data object the agent acted on**.
2. Return here, verify the eight Agent Specification properties **and the Six Questions on every data object** for every agent in the same class as the one that failed; score the **Four Pillars** for the affected agent class and bring each pillar to ≥ 3 before redeployment. For board reporting, use the Sarbanes-Oxley Moment frame and the outage-financialization language.

### Tokenmaxxing Recovery Workflow (new in v24)
When AI spend is high, individual productivity is up, and firm-level ROI is flat:
1. Start here, run the **Tokenmaxxing Test** (Leaderboard / Geometry / Latency) from `templates/rewrite-readiness-scorecard.md`.
2. Kill any input-side proxy metrics and leaderboards on the spot; they are paying for Goodhart's Law.
3. Route the fix to the decision layer, not the tool layer: Chapter 6 (exception architecture, congestion collapse) and REWRITE Step 4 (Diagnose & Strip) via `templates/task-decomposition-matrix.md`.
4. Return here, re-run the Dabbling Test after one quarter; the Jenkins metric (volume of workflows safely executed by autonomous agents under human command) replaces the retired proxies.

### Mission-Driven Adaptation Workflow
When the entity is government, non-profit, or other mission-driven (immune-system-is-law):
1. Start here, confirm Edge Mode is the only path; Direct Mode does not exist.
2. Adjust REWRITE Steps 1–6 for procurement timelines, civil service protections, and codified compliance. Use the UAE Sovereign Stack Playbook sequence (`[SOVEREIGN_STACK_PLAYBOOK]`, Chapter 11) as the reference architecture; adapt, do not copy.
3. Frame the citizen demand forcing function (Singapore Ask Jamie, UK Bobbi, US municipal permit acceleration, UAE).
4. Return here, name the sovereign-AI requirement (own the inference, the orchestration logic, and the fine-tuning data).
