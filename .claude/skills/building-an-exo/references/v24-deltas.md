## The Tokenmaxxing Collapse (Chapter 1, verbatim core)

"The behavioral anchor arrived in late May 2026, when the dabbling era began collapsing in public. Only **27% of executives say AI has met their ROI expectations** (Oliver Wyman Forum, *CEO Agenda 2026*). Inside AI-forward incumbents, the 'tokenmaxxing' play—track token usage as a proxy for AI productivity, leaderboard it, reward it—imploded inside a single quarter." Meta took down its internal token leaderboard. Microsoft cancelled Claude Code subscriptions for several product divisions. Uber's COO disclosed the company burned its entire 2026 token budget in four months. Salesforce's Marc Benioff put his firm's annual Anthropic bill at roughly $300M and asked for a "smart router." Amazon employees spun up agents on meaningless tasks to keep their stats up. Verbatim close: **"Individual productivity is up. Firm-level ROI is not. The proxy failed because the architecture beneath it did not change."**

## The Tokenmaxxing Test (Appendix A, new diagnostic)

"Operational companion to the Dabbling Test." Three checks; a single Yes places the firm below L3 regardless of spend:

1. **Leaderboard:** any function rewarding token usage or input-side proxies (paying directly for Goodhart's Law).
2. **Geometry:** agents preserving the existing org chart, approval chain, and workflow boundaries ("group drive on a steam-era shop floor").
3. **Latency:** customer-signal-to-shipped-change not 5x faster despite 5x faster tasks (congestion).

Verbatim: **"Three Yeses, or three Don't-Knows, equal transformation theater regardless of spend."** The fix lives in Chapter 6 and REWRITE Step 4.

## The Dabbling Test, Restored in Full + the Jenkins Convergence (Appendix A)

Two checks, both must pass: the 50% Time Check and the Operating-Cadence Check. Krivkovich anchor retained: *"If 50% of my time isn't spent differently because I can access AI to do my job, I'm dabbling."* New convergence paragraph, verbatim core: Tom Jenkins (OpenText) makes the same move, "stop measuring how many employees use AI assistants, and start measuring the volume of workflows safely executed by autonomous agents under human command. When the enterprise-software establishment and McKinsey converge on the exact same metric, the metric is real."

## The Vendor Shortcut (Chapter 8, new sidebar)

SAP's "Autonomous Enterprise" (Sapphire, May 12, 2026): 50+ Joule Assistants, 200+ agents, €100M partner fund. Three structural reasons buying the suite is not the rewrite: **Group Drive vs. Unit Drive** ("A rented agent catalog cannot redesign your floor boundaries"), **Utility Bill vs. Value Moat** (identical vendor catalogs are a software utility bill, not a moat), and **Cognitive Captivity** (one vendor supplying systems of record, orchestrators, and governance binds your autonomy ceiling to their roadmap). Directive: "Buy the vendor suite if it makes your legacy mothership cheaper to operate. Do not confuse it with the structural rewrite of your operating model." Falsifiable 2028 bet, verbatim: **"We are betting on the edge; legacy suites are betting you will never change the org chart. One of us is wrong."** SAP doubles as validation of the destination (Appendix C close: its flagship autonomous assistant is the financial close) and dispute of the route.

## The Binding Problem and Retention-by-Resonance (Chapter 3, new SHAPE-H subsection)

Verbatim: "When AI collapses coordination cost, it collapses exit cost in the exact same motion. A high-judgment human can leave and reconstitute an entire operating context, agents and tools included, in days. Retention-by-friction is dead."

Verbatim: "You bind high-judgment talent by giving their choices the largest possible surface area to matter." Three forces: **Consequence** (one person's judgment governs a fleet of agents), **Legibility** (visibility of who decided what must be engineered), **Identity** (a purpose specific enough to exclude). Compensation is hygiene: pay to parity, then stop competing there. Chapter 6 ties the Pod Leader's 50× leverage to this as the retention lever.

## The Agentic Fidelity Paradox and the Third Purpose Litmus Test (Chapter 3)

Verbatim: the Agentic Fidelity Paradox is "the more precisely agents adhere to predefined procedure, the less capable they become on novel problems" (Delphi Group, 2026). The Purpose Litmus Tests are now three; the new third: *"Could a high-judgment human, reading only your Identity Layer, answer why they stay, what their contribution makes visible, and who the organization is not for?"* The Identity Layer now carries explicit disqualifiers. (Sources note: Delphi Group's competing five-level Readiness Hierarchy and 11-skills taxonomy were "evaluated and not adopted.")

## The Ju Coordination Tax and Congestion (Chapters 2 and 6)

Verbatim (Ch 2): "One proof says coordination got cheap. The other says most of it was never needed." Harang Ju (Johns Hopkins, 2026) applies the CALM theorem: coordination is required for correctness only when new information can retract prior conclusions; **74% of 65 APQC enterprise workflows are monotonic** (provably executable without any coordination mechanism); 42% on the O*NET replication; borderline cases classified conservatively. Verbatim (Ch 6): "Ju's 'Coordination Tax,' the share of coordination spending that buys no correctness at all, runs 24–57%." Caveat: monotonicity guarantees correctness, not quality.

Congestion (Azhar), verbatim: "the buildup of accelerated individual work waiting on an unchanged decision layer." Congestion is the operational fingerprint of middle management as coordination cost.

## The Delayering Steelman (Chapter 6, new section)

Alloy Partners (2026): middle managers do invisible work; cut the layer and the damage arrives as an innovation collapse on a 2–3 year lag; the 1990s reengineering wave is the record. The book's answer: translation work migrates into INTERPRET, integration into ORCHESTRATE, and tacit knowledge into the Elicitation Apprenticeship, Junior Loop, and Bridge Curriculum. Verbatim: **"Delayering without the Stack is the Alloy scenario. Delayering after the Stack absorbs the coordination is this chapter."** The difference from 1990s reengineering is the order of operations.

## The Accounting Precedent (Chapter 2, new arc)

Pacioli 1494 → VisiCalc 1979 → bookkeepers lifted from posting to judging. Verbatim: **"The machine took the posting. The human took the judgment. That is the gatekeeper-to-validator shift, and it is not a forecast."** Warning carried with it: automating every entry-level posting removes the rung where future controllers learned judgment; the path upward has to be engineered (Junior Loop, Bridge Curriculum).

## The Electrification Precedent (Chapter 2, new)

Paul David (1990): general-purpose technologies depress measured productivity until the org is rewritten around them; factories took ~40 years from electric lighting through **group drive** (motors on steam-era drive shafts) to **unit drive** (one motor per machine, reorganized floor), and only the last step produced the 5.4%-per-year boom of 1919–29. Group drive vs. unit drive is the load-bearing metaphor behind both the Tokenmaxxing Geometry check and the Vendor Shortcut.

## The Two J-Curves (Chapter 12, new distinction)

The **productivity J-curve** (Brynjolfsson; Paul David; Azhar) is the macro dip until industries reorganize. The **dual-cost J-curve** is the firm-level P&L trough during edge deployment, when legacy and AI-native operations run concurrently ($2–5M Year 1 for a $500M firm; first deprecation Month 9–12; cost crossover Month 18–30). Verbatim directive: **"Pre-fund the firm-level trough; cite the macro trough to manage investor and board expectations."**

## Phantom Jobs (Chapter 12, promoted to header term)

Verbatim definition: phantom jobs are "roles that Okun's Law would have predicted given current GDP growth but that were never created, because corporate output now flows through intelligence systems before reaching labor markets." Shrier (Imperial College, 2026): ~19M phantom jobs in the US, 9M in Europe; Stanford/Dallas Fed corroborate at entry level (13% employment decline for ages 22–25 in high-exposure occupations since 2022); projected structural 155-million job shortfall across US and European OECD economies over a decade. **Cognitive blocs** (US, China, EU, India) bolded in the geopolitical section.

## The Domain Collapse Engine (Chapter 11, promoted to thesis)

## Spatial vs. Organizational World Models (Chapters 2 and 12)

Two senses of "world model" by 2026, and the book uses only one. The AI field's sense is spatial (Fei-Fei Li's World Labs, $1B; LeCun's AMI Labs, $1.03B; over $2B in seed across three weeks of early 2026; KION + Accenture training autonomous forklift fleets for GXO inside warehouse twins; NVIDIA's $1T+ industrial digital twin market). The book uses Block's organizational sense: the firm's continuously updated representation of its own operations (INTERPRET + LEARN). Both descend from Kenneth Craik (1943). Verbatim: **"The field is building Craik's models of space. This book is about the firm becoming Craik's model of itself."** Distinction to hold: an industrial digital twin simulates a *facility*; the Edge Twin replicates a *business function*.

## How to Apply This Reference

1. **Board governance conversation.** Lead with the Sarbanes-Oxley Moment, then the outage financialization frame, then the Four Pillars as the implementation. The board hears fiduciary duty, not engineering.
2. **Vendor-suite pressure.** When SAP (or any suite vendor) is on the table, run the Vendor Shortcut triad: group drive vs. unit drive, utility bill vs. value moat, cognitive captivity. Close with the 2028 falsifiable bet.
3. **Tokenmaxxing intake.** When a firm reports high AI spend with flat ROI, run Leaderboard / Geometry / Latency before anything else. Three Yeses or three Don't-Knows: route to Chapter 6 and REWRITE Step 4, not to a new tool purchase.
4. **Retention conversation.** When a leadership team treats retention as a compensation problem, deliver the Binding Problem: exit cost collapsed with coordination cost; engineer Consequence, Legibility, and Identity; pay to parity, then stop.
5. **Delayering pressure.** When the board wants headcount cuts before the Stack exists, deliver the Alloy steelman and the order-of-operations answer verbatim.
6. **CFO conversation.** Replace the retired SemiAnalysis figures with the operational test: can the CFO calculate the exact cost of inference per completed workflow? If not, the architecture is not operationalized.
