## How being unaffiliated changes the plan

1. **Sequence: accuracy review → curated DOI → targeted outreach.** A Zenodo DOI gives persistence,
   citation, and attribution — it is **not** peer review, validation, or institutional legitimacy, so do
   not treat it as a credential. First send a private (or clearly "pre-release, not yet public") link to
   the source authors whose results are uncertain — **especially Dr. Pauli for the unconfirmed USZ
   compound identities** — and correct anything they flag. Then mint a **curated, versioned** DOI (a
   dedicated atlas subset, not the whole repository; see `DEPOSIT.md`). Then do targeted outreach.
2. **The artifact carries what legitimacy it can — used honestly.** Get a free ORCID (orcid.org) and
   cite the DOI once minted. But lead every contact with a single, specific, low-commitment ask, not with
   claims about the work's importance.
3. **Run the source-author and foundation tracks in parallel (after correction).** "Foundation-first" is
   not inherently superior. A concise source-author accuracy check builds credibility; foundations can
   help identify eligible labs or funding mechanisms. One polite follow-up only, after ~10–14 days.
4. **Recruit an eligible collaborator honestly.** An institutional PI would lead any grant under the
   funder's rules; authorship follows substantive contribution (ICMJE/COPE). Do not offer open-ended
   labor, and do not imply a partnership before it exists.
5. **Expect a numbers game.** Unaffiliated cold-email reply rates are low even done well.
